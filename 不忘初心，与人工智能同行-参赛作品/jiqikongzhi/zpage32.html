Robotic "unmanned restaurant" liberation labor

More and more traditional enterprises are constantly looking for a combination with new industries, while traditional catering enterprises face the challenges of increasing labor costs year by year, large mobility, and loss of service personnel. From the perspective of labor cost and time cost, both the feeding robot and the cooking robot can perform long-term repetitive work, which can solve the dilemma of traditional catering enterprises recruiting people and losing people.

Four graduates from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology opened the world's first robotic restaurant in Boston, from ordering to cooking, loading, and dishwashing, all done by robots, completely liberating the workforce.

With the popularity of unmanned retail, more and more retail stores are getting rid of the clerk, so can the restaurant be unmanned? The four graduates of the Massachusetts Institute of Technology made this wish a reality. They opened the world's first robotic restaurant in Boston. From ordering to cooking, loading and dishwashing, all were done by robots and completely liberated. labor force.

Entering the restaurant called Spyce, there is no waiter smiling, and all the ordering work needs to be done by the customer. There is a row of self-service ordering machines at the door, where customers can order food on the screen. In the transparent kitchen next to it, there are nearly 10 robot chefs. Each robot chef has a circular screen above it to show the name of the dishes.

Once the order information is received, the robot chef starts working. According to the recipe, the ingredients needed for cooking will be sent to the conveyor belt, and the robot chef will pour all the ingredients into a cylindrical automatic wok.

Unlike human cooking, robots cook more intelligently, and the automatic wok that collects ingredients will roll 360 degrees like a drum washing machine. In order to allow customers to see the cooking process of the food, the automatic wok is not placed vertically, but tilted to an angle that is easy for everyone to see. When the meal is done, the automatic wok will tilt down and pour the prepared food into the bowl.

These automatic woks have a strict temperature control system that allows the food to be evenly heated without worrying about the heat. Every dish on the menu is also set with precise cooking time and temperature programs, and sensors are installed in the kitchen to monitor the food temperature to ensure no mistakes.

Above each robot chef, a screen is installed, and the customer can take the menu according to the menu information displayed to the corresponding window. However, the robot restaurant is not completely unoccupied, and the restaurant hired a shop assistant to decorate the dishes for the customers. When the customer picks up the meal, the only clerk in the store can add ingredients such as sauces and parsley to the customer.

How did the robot restaurant appear? In fact, the four founders are also full of food. They were members of the water polo team during their studies at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology. They were physically active and consumed more nutritious food. Because the meals in the school were too expensive, they had the idea of ​​designing a robotic restaurant.

In 2015, they successfully completed the first robotic restaurant model in an entrepreneurial project at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology. After graduating in 2016, they did not give up this dream, successfully got the investment, and finally commercialized the student project in May this year. The Spyce Robot Restaurant opened in the golden section of Boston and officially met with you.

This restaurant is not only novel and interesting, but also very cheap. Due to the reduced labor costs, the cost of a healthy meal has been reduced from $12 to $7.5. The cooking efficiency of the robot is also very fast. According to the calculation, the whole process is no more than 3 minutes from the start of the order to the meal.

This is a fast-food restaurant that is convenient for the economy. It also invited a Michelin star chef to be the cooking director. Each dish has four options: “regular”, “vegetarian (can have fish)”, “no wheat bran” and “strict vegetarian”.

The founder of the robotic restaurant once said that as an engineer obsessed with robots, he hopes to create a new and efficient cooking method. However, this unmanned restaurant will also test the quality of the customer. What if the customer took the wrong item or did not send the used tableware to the designated location? The robot restaurant may have to work hard on customer education.
