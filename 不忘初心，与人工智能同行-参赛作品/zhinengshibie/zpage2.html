Trend Analysis: Application and Challenges of Artificial Intelligence in the Field of Communication Services

Figure 1. Application and challenges of artificial intelligence in the field of communication services

With the continuous improvement of computer processing power and the latest developments in the field of cloud storage, the sci-fi concept of artificial intelligence (AI) is becoming an attractive reality. Many industries are currently exploring how to make better use of artificial intelligence, and the telecommunications industry is no exception.

A recent survey by the TM Forum showed that in the telecommunications industry, there is a strong desire to start implementing artificial intelligence (AI) solutions, but the telecommunications industry has not reached a consensus on the best path to implement artificial intelligence (AI). From network management to predictive maintenance, there are some fledgling AI application cases, but I have to admit that the telecom industry faces many challenges in adopting AI.

Figure 2. Most network failures are caused by humans.

With the help of AI products and services, some CSPs (Communications Service Providers) have begun to implement artificial intelligence (AI) solutions in a variety of ways. Some of these services are designed to transform the Network Operations Center (NOC), which is managed by a human administrator, into a Service Operations Center (SOC), where AI provides analysis and closed-loop automation. For example, Telefonica launched the SOC pilot in several markets.

Figure 3. Network failure affects many people.

Other CSPs are using artificial intelligence (AI) in different ways. For example, AT&T is investigating how to use artificial intelligence (AI) algorithms to enable drones to check and repair base stations. South Korea's SK Telecom is using machine learning, a common artificial intelligence (AI), to analyze network traffic to detect anomalies and enhance network operations. Hong Kong Telecom PCCW is testing artificial intelligence-driven tools to predict network capacity growth and predict network failures.

Opportunities for artificial intelligence (AI) in telecommunications

Although CSP (Communications Service Providers) has begun to explore the potential of artificial intelligence (AI), there are still many opportunities to take advantage of. For example, an artificial intelligence (AI) solution can be used to help develop a business strategy. This can identify market trends, support operational decisions, predict future trends, and more by generating ideas for products and services.

Figure 4. Perceptual radio is a learning network

There are two broad categories of opportunities for artificial intelligence (AI) to provide unparalleled opportunities: network management and operations, and customer focus. Clearly, there are actually many possible requirements for network management that can benefit from artificial intelligence (AI) solutions to cope with the rapidly expanding number of connected devices and users. Even the customer-oriented CSP (Communications Service Providers) terminal services can benefit greatly from artificial intelligence (AI) technology, as customers will increasingly need personalized services.

Some of the customer-centric approaches to improving customer care through artificial intelligence (AI) involve smart customer care. Data and artificial intelligence (AI) analysis can be used to help customers with billing, device access, troubleshooting and other services to prevent customer churn. Artificial intelligence (AI) can also be used for smart marketing, personalized reward strategies to win customers, determine platform engagement, OTT upsells, and more.

Figure 5. Next-generation networks will use artificial intelligence technology.

Chatbots are the ultimate customer-centric artificial intelligence (AI) solution. Chatbots are automated customer support that intelligently solves problems without the need for an operator. Chatbots benefit CSPs, which reduce the number of employees required and provide customers with personalized help immediately. Thirty percent of the CSPs surveyed by TM Forum said they have deployed Chatbots, and one-seventh of CSPs have redeployed some customer service agents to perform higher-value tasks.

Some CSPs, including SK Telecom, Telefónica and Orange, have begun to move chat bots by introducing voice assistants. Consumers are already familiar with the concept of voice assistants and are quickly accustomed to Siri, Alexa, Google Assistant and other intelligent voice assistant systems. For CSP, Voice Assistant can provide customers with the highest level of personalized and friendly support.

Implementing the challenge of artificial intelligence (AI)

Although artificial intelligence (AI) has many benefits and CSPs (Communications Service Providers) are eager to get them, it is difficult for CSPs to launch effective AI solutions so far. Because artificial intelligence technology is relatively immature, it affects a series of implementations of all relevant industries and has some challenges, especially the challenges faced by the telecommunications industry.

Perhaps the most important obstacle to AI adoption is the lack of maturity of the technology. As the number of business intelligence solutions continues to increase, it is difficult to keep up with the best solutions for business development. In fact, 66% of CSP respondents said that one of the three major barriers to their application in artificial intelligence is lack of maturity.

Another major obstacle to artificial intelligence (AI) is the lack of expertise in artificial intelligence (AI) software and data analysis. Although the limited number of AI experts poses a challenge to all industries, the telecommunications industry may be particularly vulnerable to this problem. Without proper incentives, computer scientists and data analysts may prefer other industries to apply their skills. This may mean that CSP will eventually have to find partners to help launch their artificial intelligence (AI) solutions.

Figure 6. Communication service data information can be used to optimize the network.

Many other concerns have hampered the adoption of AI. If control is in the hands of artificial intelligence algorithms, 43% of CSPs are concerned about losing control of their networks. 37% of CSPs are concerned about the lack of standards in AI technology. Finally, 32% of CSPs are concerned about the possibility of employee turnover.

Artificial intelligence (AI) adoption strategy

Because the potential benefits of artificial intelligence are as important as they are, CSPs must adopt artificial intelligence solutions as early as possible. By entering artificial intelligence doors as early as possible, CSPs can seize competitive positions in a changing telecommunications environment. Fortunately, CSPs can use many strategies to get started with AI technology.

Figure 7. Cellular network-based car networking

First, the CSP should know as much as possible about the current possibilities of AI technology. This may require finding potential partners, experimenting with artificial intelligence solutions, and joining standards organizations and other relevant agencies.

CSPs should also pave the way for AI solutions by reflecting on their data. Data is the seed of artificial intelligence, so CSPs should do everything they can to remove silos and open data resources through APIs. Through standardized data collection and management, CSP will better conduct business with AI partners and begin to use AI effectively.

Figure 8. Artificial intelligence-based network architecture

Finally, CSPs should welcome AI collaborators and open themselves up for new experimental solutions. While there is no need to work with any particular partner, it's important to keep open options and explore the possibilities of various AIs. CSPs shouldn't forget that AI is still in its infancy, and exploring multiple options is the best way to find the best for your business.
