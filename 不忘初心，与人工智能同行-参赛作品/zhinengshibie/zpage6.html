U.S. uses artificial intelligence for satellite remote sensing image recognition

Researchers at the University of Missouri in the United States have published a deep learning model for remote sensing satellite image recognition in a recent issue of Applied Remote Sensing, which is said to be able to identify and mark military equipment from massive satellite images. The researchers also converted the identified original images into training samples for the deep learning model to continue data training to improve the accuracy of target recognition.

For a long time, intelligence agencies have relied mainly on intelligence analysts to identify targets such as nuclear facilities or secret military positions from satellite remote sensing images, but artificial identification faces inefficiencies and the inability to cope with massive data. With the maturity of artificial intelligence technology represented by deep learning, it has become a development trend to introduce it into remote sensing satellite target recognition. In July 2017, the US Intelligence Advanced Research Projects Agency launched the "World Functional Map Challenge" to seek artificial intelligence solutions to better extract high-value information from massive satellite images, and strive to reduce the workload of intelligence analysts by 75%. Digital Earth has also developed the Geospatial Big Data platform, seeking to use deep learning algorithms to automatically detect and identify high-value targets.

At present, the use of artificial intelligence to carry out satellite image recognition still faces many challenges. On the one hand, the image of remote sensing satellites is more complex and susceptible to weather, imaging angle, sensor type, and camouflage concealment. On the other hand, deep learning algorithms rely on a large amount of satellite image data, but it is often difficult to obtain such data to accurately identify features of satellite images.
