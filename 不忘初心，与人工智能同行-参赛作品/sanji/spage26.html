Technology to make up for the lack of manpower, Japan sent AI, robots to guard the homework

For the international sports events like the Olympics, the most important thing besides the preparation of the stadium, the guards and the Wei'an are also unspeakable areas. However, in the face of tens of thousands of watchers and passengers, how can Japan cope with the shortage of manpower? The answer is to make up for AI, robots and other high-tech to make up.

According to the Asahi Shimbun, the Japan Police Agency is working with Panasonic to develop a dedicated AI system for the 2020 Tokyo Olympics, and to conduct a survey at the Sumida River Fireworks Conference, which has reached 870,000 people.

In this experiment, Panasonic set the camera on a police car parked at two major intersections, allowing AI to learn the flow of data from crowds. The goal is to let the AI ​​learn to discern suspicious behaviors of humans or vehicles, such as standing still for a long time, moving against the flow of people, placing suspicious items, and driving into restricted areas.

In fact, the shortage of guards has occurred in the past 2012 London Olympics and the 2016 Rio Olympics. To this end, Japan, a technology power, will hand over this burden to AI and robots. In the future, the system will continue to be used on a large scale to accumulate data and finally take the lead in the 2020 Olympics.

Panasonic said that because the system is still under development, it is not daring to guarantee whether it will catch up with 2020. However, since the taxpayer’s money has been collected, it is bound to go all out and ask for more results in the future.

In addition, the guard robot is also considered to be a solution to the shortage of manpower.
