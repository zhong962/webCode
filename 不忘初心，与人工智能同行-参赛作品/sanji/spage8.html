2018 college entrance examination survey: more than 60% of the trend of professional management of artificial intelligence, etc.

After the results of the college entrance examinations are announced each year, many candidates will choose to be professionally guilty. So, do they have any troubles in this area, and which majors do they prefer?

After the results of the national college entrance examination in 2018, we sent a “2018 college entrance examination questionnaire survey” to more than 60 candidates from 31 provinces across the country. A total of 27 valid questionnaires were collected, covering college entrance examinations in 19 provinces. .

According to the above survey, nearly 50% of the respondents who participated in the volunteers were ranked in the professional rankings, while 30% of the interviewed candidates were most concerned with the school rankings. The other candidates who were interviewed were most concerned about the city and environment. Nearly 75% of the candidates interviewed have clear professional goals, and the rest of the candidates are hesitant in professional selection.

In addition, more than 60% of the interviewed candidates tend to apply for economics, management and so on. New engineering majors such as artificial intelligence are also highly regarded by candidates. More than half of the candidates surveyed “will consider and would like to apply for a new engineering major”.

In terms of professionalism, Yu Chu (pseudonym), a candidate from the Northwest Territories, tends to be in economic management. "Managing wealth is more interesting than earning other people's wealth." Yu Chu believes that the management profession "communicates with people", parents do not want him to be a nerd. "To be more in touch with people, several of my friends are also this major."

According to the survey, the popular majors selected by the candidates are mainly “economics” and “management”. Among the multiple-choice questions in the questionnaire about “what kind of specialty is preferred”, 44.44% of the candidates interviewed Economics was chosen, and the number of candidates who chose to manage the program also reached 22.22%.

Tianjin Science candidate Zhou Yan said that he has received the admission letter from Peking University and started school in September. She will officially become one of the freshmen of Peking University Guanghua School of Management. “When I saw some books on economics and finance in the third day of junior high school, I began to be interested in finance.” Zhou Yan said frankly that when the dream came into reality, the moment he received the acceptance letter, he was very excited.

Peng Yuxin (a pseudonym), a liberal arts candidate in a province in the north, chose the Peking University School of Economics, claiming that this choice belongs to “mistaken mistakes”. "At the beginning, it was quite common, because I didn't have a clear preference." Peng Yuxin said that after consulting Peking University's enrollment teachers and seniors, he felt that economics was "very appetizing," and he decided.

In her view, this subject is a way of thinking, using data, models, reasoning, and analysis to rationally interpret the way the objective world operates, revealing the essence through the appearance of seemingly different things. "As a 'atypical liberal arts student', the rigor, accuracy and rationality of the economy is quite consistent with me." Peng Yuxin said that he "does enough of the essays" and prefers to "strongly argue and persuade" himself. thing.

Literary science from Liaoning, Tianshi, successfully entered the Guanghua School of Management at Tsinghua University to study economic management. Shi Tianle told the news that many students joked that "the test into Guanghua is falling into the eyes of money", he believes that this is the "stereotype" of the society for related professions.

For him, the economic conditions at home are acceptable, and he is not in a hurry to make money. "I would like to do something to serve the society and do some industry. But to do business, it is necessary to have a knowledge base and financial support. It is useless to rely on mouth guns." Shi Tianle thinks that he has "the economy and the people" Ambition, willing to "do things in a down-to-earth manner", and entering Tsinghua is a good starting point. "I use my own economic knowledge to support the development of some emerging industries. This is what I yearn for."

I hope to become a "composite talent"

Unlike other well-specified candidates, Liu Meng, a Jiangxi liberal arts candidate, chose to enroll in the Xinya College of Tsinghua University, leaving a "suspense" for his college career.

Xinya Academy is a special “Institute of Accommodation and Liberal Arts” established by Tsinghua University in 2014 to explore the reform and innovation of undergraduate education. Students are not required to be professional at the time of enrollment. They first accept small class general education based on mathematics, humanities and social sciences. After that, you can freely choose the various professional directions of Tsinghua University (except for individual majors such as clinical medicine), or choose interdisciplinary development.

"Xinya College is at the forefront of education reform. It conforms to the trend of no clues, let you explore and cultivate your own interests, and then choose the direction to go in the future." Liu Meng said that as far as her understanding is concerned, the society needs more now. Many "composite talents", if they limit themselves to the liberal arts, will narrow the path of their future development. "The liberal arts is the way I want to go in the future, but in the liberal arts study, I also use the science of thinking to expand the angle. I choose Tsinghua Xinya, that is, choose the challenge, escape the ease track and inertia thinking."

Anhui Science and Technology Bafang Qingyuan also hopes to become a "composite talent." According to his introduction, his father is a computer technician in the local tax system. For the current hot engineering major, his father suggested that he should not choose a pure computer major. Fang Qingyuan is quite in favor of this, and he is more willing to become a cross-disciplinary talent in multiple fields.

Zhang Qian (pseudonym) from Yunnan was admitted to the Chinese Department of Peking University this year, but she hopes that the school's "general education" concept will enable her to develop in an all-round way. "What I lack is to use science and engineering knowledge and thinking to consider problems. It is often sensuous, easy-going, and not rational." Zhang Qian hopes to accept general education and take a basic course in science to "cultivate certain rationality." thinking". "Undergraduate students should focus on popularizing general education and cultivating comprehensive and interdisciplinary and diverse talents. This should be the goal of undergraduate education. Graduate students and doctoral students are biased towards academic research."

New engineering majors such as artificial intelligence are receiving attention

Not long ago, the Ministry of Education issued the "Notice on the publication of the first batch of "new engineering" research and practice projects". A total of 612 projects from universities across the country were selected, including popular professional direction such as artificial intelligence, big data, and intelligent manufacturing. 19 project groups are covered. Relevant majors are also highly regarded by candidates. More than half of the candidates interviewed “will consider and would like to apply for a new engineering major”.

Guizhou science examiner Yan Hongwei said that his target professional direction is "computer", because he has been interested in "electronic technology" since he was a senior. According to its introduction, he won the provincial second prize of the Robotics Engineering Competition of Guizhou Province and the second prize of the provincial high school group of the first open source electronic design competition in Guizhou Province.

Yan Hongwei’s idol is Nan Rendong, a science and technology worker who died of illness. "He has lung cancer but has been conducting research in the mountainous areas of Guizhou for more than a decade. He likes this spirit very much and contributes to the country. He hopes to become such a person in the future." Yan Hongwei said.

In the survey, 33.33% of the respondents indicated that they are very interested in the artificial intelligence industry and plan to engage in related industries.

Yan Hongwei said that he usually pays more attention to "artificial intelligence" and hopes to develop in this field in the future. "The current 'artificial intelligence' is flawed, it is difficult to have real emotions like human beings, I hope to break through this flaw." Yan Hongwei told the news that he is very interested in the competition and hopes to be friendly with outstanding students in the university. Do more research projects with teachers.

In Liu Meng's view, as a major focus of the current national education construction, the "new engineering" heat is inevitable because of the high social demand, good employment and high wages. "New engineering with more emphasis on practicality will also promote the reform of the education system, and further promote the need for cross-disciplinary integration, because whether it is artificial intelligence or cloud computing, or the upgrading of traditional engineering, it is no longer a professional. Completed."

Selecting literature and history subjects based on love

The liberal arts disciplines such as philosophy, literature, and history have not been too neglected by the school tyrants. More than 40% of the surveyed candidates tend to apply for relevant majors.

The liberal arts master Zhou Junrou from Guangxi had a special liking for the Chinese department of Peking University and was successfully admitted. "I originally wanted to report on philosophy, I feel that I will be pressing online Peking University. Later, the scores are higher, and the Chinese department can also report. After entering the university, I am not ready to give up philosophy. I will consider minors in the future." Zhou Junrou told Yu News.

Zhou Junrou's idol is the writer "August Changan", who studied at Peking University Guanghua School of Management. Zhou Junrou took the above road and chose Peking University Chinese major to be affected to some extent. "The first choice for the liberal arts is because my academic performance is quite good. After reading her book, I was affected. Some important characters in her novels are those who chose liberal arts. At that time, some teachers persuaded me to say that my science is also very good, but choose There is no right or wrong." Zhou Junrou said.

For the future, Zhou Junrou said that "I have never thought of becoming a writer." "I don't want to use my identity as a home to frame myself. I usually write something when I want to write something. I am more willing to study than writing a book. I want to do academics in the future. Even though I am still in the society, I still want to keep a copy. Longing for."

Zhang Qian told Yu News that attending the Chinese Department of Peking University is her dream of persisting in her childhood. "I am hoping that I will go to Peking University Guanghua School of Management, but I am not interested." Zhang Qian said.

In her view, "the Chinese department can't adapt to the hot industry now" is a misunderstanding. "Even "artificial intelligence" can't rely on English alone, but also Chinese. But Chinese is more complicated than English, and applied linguistics talents are equally scarce for artificial intelligence development." Zhang Qian believes that Chinese graduates are not only engaged in graduation. Journalists, literary criticism, teachers and other professions, the profession can adapt to all walks of life. “Chinese majors can train my thinking and thinking ability, no matter what career I am going to do, I will be very helpful.”

In addition, Zhang Qian is also very interested in history. "I want to learn the pre-Qin literature, but literature can not be separated from the times to study separately, so I plan to study Chinese and history after entering the university." Zhang Qian said.
