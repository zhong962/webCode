Artificial intelligence has become the driving force of enterprise development

When a new concept becomes a viable business tool, many companies will actively adopt this technology to join the market trend. This includes artificial intelligence (AI) and machine learning (ML). From high-tech giants with hundreds of years of history to startups committed to innovation, organizations of all sizes are actively investing time and resources to accelerate technology development and use it for business development.

But artificial intelligence is more than just a popular fashion. Analyst Tractica expects global companies' spending on artificial intelligence to grow from $644 million in 2016 to nearly $39 billion in 2025, and will be an efficient sales platform and virtual digital receptionist, children's toys, and self-driving cars. The driving force for the development of various aspects such as products or services.

Artificial intelligence (AI) and machine learning (ML) will ultimately provide the driving force for most companies. What kind of driving force does artificial intelligence have? That is powerful data and processing power.

Great potential, huge limitations

The potential impact of artificial intelligence on vertical industries and every business should not be underestimated. As unassisted machine learning, natural language processing (NLP), and deep learning capabilities increase, the application of each skill will continue to grow and expand into new use cases.

Many companies are already investigating how to use artificial intelligence (AI) and machine learning (ML) technologies for object recognition and tracking, localizing geographic data, preventing fraud, improving marketing results, and many other applications. While vendors in these areas want to use this technology to achieve this promise, other companies have applied these innovations to practical applications of autonomous vehicles, call centers, customer service, and network security.

Companies that have adopted artificial intelligence technology have been systematically and strategically summarizing data for many years. They are already ahead of organizations that are just beginning to focus on data collection and organization. But they also face the biggest limitation of artificial intelligence (AI) and machine learning (ML) technology: capacity.

Power, capacity, and speed are critical to smart technology

An artificial neural network (ANN) that drives the development of artificial intelligence (AI) and machine learning (ML) aims to model and process the relationship between input and output in parallel. To do this, they need to store a large amount of input data and need large-scale calculations to understand these relationships and provide the appropriate output.

Consider deploying a chat bot to provide customer self-service and assist the contact center's customer service agent team. Ideally, robots can answer questions accurately, direct customers to the right resources, and often interact with customers in a natural way.

To achieve this goal, the back end of the robot needs to quickly compare the query to the dictionary used by the corporate consumer group (ie their native language) to “understand” the context of the interaction and “take decisions” based on these inputs, hopefully Be able to react correctly and execute as immediately as humans do.

However, the processor and memory resources (DRAM) required by these processes consume a lot of bandwidth beyond the bandwidth that most on-premise data center network designs can handle. They also add considerable power overhead because the number of CPUs or GPUs involved is far greater than the amount that most organizations are prepared for. And trying to do everything in a single data center introduces latency issues that can damage the product or hinder anything the application tries to accomplish. So what should companies do?

Maximize the performance of artificial intelligence through direct cloud connectivity

Organizations that use process-intensive artificial intelligence applications are increasingly turning to hybrid deployment-ready edge data centers to address bandwidth and computing challenges, reduce operational costs, and eliminate latency issues.

What you need to do to mix and deploy a data center is:

Provides a simple entry point for cloud computing providers within the facility to significantly reduce latency and data transfer costs. Compared to the global Internet, direct cloud interconnect products can reduce latency and reduce data transfer costs without the need to manually provide private WAN connectivity to each provider.

Very close to the cloud computing provider's core compute nodes to further reduce latency between the dedicated environment and the chosen cloud computing provider. Keep performance and reliability as close as possible to the end user and device as close as possible to the user or device. This is especially beneficial for supporting delay-sensitive artificial intelligence applications such as autonomous vehicles or network security operations, while maximizing workload flexibility and cost management.

A scalable and configurable central infrastructure to promote sustainable growth.

The application of artificial intelligence and machine learning technology in people's daily lives has matured and become more and more common. As they do, companies that offer these products and services need to think strategically about how best to balance the various business needs and unlock the full potential of their technology to maintain a competitive advantage.
