Google released several AI tools: helping the public to make better use of artificial intelligence

Google announced a series of AI-related features and services at this week's Cloud Next conference, including a variety of machine learning tools, customer service tools, and productivity tools G Suite feature upgrades. Many of these features are designed to better promote artificial intelligence tools.

Machine learning development tool AutoML Vision

Starting today, AutoML Vision, a machine learning development tool, will launch Beta Beta, open to all organizations, businesses and researchers. AutoML Vision is a tool for the Cloud AutoML machine learning system that allows users to develop machine learning models for image and object recognition. Non-professional users only need to use these tools with a graphical interface and easy-to-understand touch methods such as drag and drop.

AutoML Vision was released with the Cloud AutoML project in January of this year, when it was the Alpha version. The Cloud AutoML system allows non-professionals who don't have machine learning skills and professional programming skills to train their own automated learning models by using existing tools on Google's cloud computing services.

AutoML Vision targets users who are less knowledgeable about artificial intelligence and machine learning techniques—they may think that artificial intelligence is useful, but there are not enough resources or technology to develop their own training models.

Cloud AutoML expands into new domains: natural language and translation

Google has launched two new Cloud AutoML domains: one for natural language and one for translation. Google's colloquial analysis and writing text dismantling capabilities form the basis of Google Assistant, and the proficiency of its artificial intelligence training translation algorithms has made Google Translate a great success in many different languages.

Of course, you can't develop models and algorithms that are as complex as Google without professional knowledge, resources, and data sets, but with these new domains, the company can simplify the startup process for custom model-based training. In fact, publishing giant Hearst is using AutoML Natural Language to help mark and organize the content of different magazines, as well as domestic and international versions of these publications. Google also provides AutoML Translation to the Japanese publisher Nikkei Group, which publishes and translates articles in many different languages ​​every day.

“Artificial intelligence can empower people, and we want to democratize this ability so that everyone and all businesses can enjoy it – from retail to agriculture, from education to health care.” Li Feifei, chief scientist of Google’s artificial intelligence, said in the statement “Artificial intelligence is no longer a niche technology – it’s a differentiating factor in every industry. We are committed to providing tools that can bring about radical change.”

Artificial intelligence customer service

In addition to the new Cloud AutoML domain, Google is also developing an artificial intelligence customer service product that can interact with users directly over the phone. Google calls this product Contact Center AI, which is bundled with the existing Dialogflow package and provides companies with tools to develop their customer service.

Industry insiders believe that Contact Center AI seems to be very similar to Duplex's model. The latter was launched by Google at the I/O conference earlier this year to provide people with their own conversational AI assistants to make appointments or to complete other daily tasks by phone. A Google spokesperson said that Contact Center AI and Duplex are two distinct products that share some of the underlying components, but the technology stack is completely different from the overall goal.

G Suite feature upgrade: AI syntax correction

In addition to artificial intelligence, Google has also upgraded many features of G Suite.

First, Google Docs provides functional updates such as grammar suggestions, using machine learning algorithms to identify grammar errors and modify comments. Google said it can understand the simple rules of use of "a" and "an", as well as more complex concepts such as clauses.

G Suite has added new tools to automatically remove malicious messages and identify users who may be infected by malicious third parties.

At the same time, G Suite enterprise users can now choose storage areas for major data, such as the US, Europe or the world.

In addition, Google launched the Smart Reply machine learning feature, which can use artificial intelligence to make a short response to the information received. This feature will be integrated into Hangouts Chat, a corporate chat platform similar to Slack. Existing G Suite customers can use the service in the coming weeks. Previously, in May, Google launched the automatic mail writing function Smart Compose for Gmail users, similar to Smart Reply.

In addition to these updates, Google pointed out that the company has added nearly 300 new features to G Suite in the past year, and currently has more than 4 million companies paying for G Suite, including well-known companies such as Whirlpool, Broadcom and PricewaterhouseCoopers. . In this week's conference call, Google CEO Sundar Pichai also revealed that G Suite's quarterly revenue exceeded $1 billion.
