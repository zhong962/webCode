The American farmer used the "automatic bird-washing laser device" to say goodbye to millions of agricultural losses.

More than 20,000 years ago, humans began farming life, and at the same time set off a battle for food and wildlife. Until now, the arbitrary plunder of "uninvited guests" is still a problem that farmers must solve. Among them, there is a farmer in the United States Blueberry Park, which has produced a "automatic bird-washing laser machine", and has since spoken to millions of farmers.

According to Gigazine, the bitter Lord is Justin Meduri, who lives in Oregon, and manages a blueberry garden. Every harvest season, there will always be 2,000 to 3,000 huge "bird army", plundering the hard-planted blueberries, causing a quarter of the farmland to be drained, with losses of up to $100,000 (equivalent) NT$3 million).

In the face of huge agricultural losses, Justin Meduri also hired his natural enemy eagle to come to the town. However, not only is the cost high, but the eagle also has the risk of going on strike and leaving, and the results are not satisfactory. Under this premise, he decided to introduce a fully automatic laser device manufactured by Bird Control Group, becoming one of the first farmers in the United States to use laser-by-bird.

In Justin's 1.35 km × 0.7 km blueberry garden, there are six lasers that look like monitors. The machine is powered by solar energy and emits irregular green laser light to scare the birds. Its bird-by-bird principle applies the instinct of birds, allowing birds to misidentify the laser as a predator, and scare away.

During the day, you can see a green spot on the ground in the farm. In fact, the entire blueberry garden is covered with a laser beam. Since laser light is dangerous to humans or wild animals, it may be blind if exposed. Therefore, the user needs to be trained and must post a "Dangerous Do Not Enter" notice.

In order to avoid harming animals, the Bird Control Group also adjusted the direction and power of laser light to solve this problem. The move also earned it recognition from the World Conservation Fund.

What is the effectiveness of the laser-by-bird? Justin Meduri said with joy that the birds that were deeply troubled in the past can't even see a feather.

The price of the fully automatic laser device is about 9,500 US dollars per unit. In the case of the blueberry garden, the equipment cost is about NT$1.75 million, and the agricultural damage is 3 million yuan, which is equivalent to saving 125. Ten thousand yuan, the benefits are extremely high.
