Google developed AI diagnostic eye disease technology, accurate and authoritative experts

Recently, an eye hospital in London and DeepMind, an AI development unit of Google, jointly announced a study that has mastered AI technology that can analyze complex eye structures. This technology can diagnose more than 50 eye diseases with accuracy comparable to authoritative experts.

The study was published in the international academic journal Nature Medicine. The researchers said they invited 1,000 eye disease patients and eight ophthalmology authoritative experts to participate in the experiment. The experiment required ophthalmology authoritative experts and AI to classify 1,000 patients into four categories: "emergency", "semi-emergency", "inspection" and "observable". The results show that the error rate of ophthalmologists and AI is about 5.5%, and the strength of the two is comparable. Both have an accuracy of 100% in the identification of the "emergency" category. The research team hopes that the technology will speed up the process of judging the condition and allow patients to receive treatment at the right time.

Currently, DeepMind is working with Imperial College London to study how to train AI to analyze mammograms to accurately diagnose breast cancer. However, there are also concerns that in the process of training AI, it is necessary to collect a large number of medical records, which will invade the privacy of patients. DeepMind researchers say they value patient privacy and emphasize that the company has the highest security system to prevent data leakage.
