US University installed 2,300 Amazon smart speakers, voice assistants to answer questions from students at any time

St. Louis University announced that it will place 2,300 Amazon-made Echo Dot voice-activated speakers in student residences. The voice-activated speakers have built-in voice assistant Alexa, which can answer questions about campus life anytime, anywhere.

According to the news, St. Louis University will place 2,300 Echo Dots in the student residences before the start of the month. What makes Echo Dot special is the ability to answer more than 100 questions related to St. Louis University. For example: "When is the library closed tonight?" or "Where is the registration office?" It also provides information about on-campus activities, such as lecturers visiting the school, concerts to be held, and so on.

As for personal privacy issues, St. Louis University refers to the Amazon voice assistant Alexa they use. Each Echo Dot is managed by a central system and is not connected to any personal account. The school said it will not collect any personal data and all users are anonymous. Echo Dots does not store any student questions. If you don't want to use it, you can unplug the power, put the device away, and return it to the school at the end of the semester.
