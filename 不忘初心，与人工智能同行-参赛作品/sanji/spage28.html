Dongda Qing should work together to develop the "fourth hand", which can be remotely controlled by others.

I have previously reported many third hands that can be manipulated by consciousness, or auxiliary machine exoskeleton, and now even the fourth hand! Recently, the University of Tokyo and Keio University have teamed up to develop a device with two arms, with a focus on remote control by others.

Generally speaking, most of the equipments installed on the body are designed to assist the user, so the user is operated by consciousness or remote control. However, the purpose of research and development of Dongda and Keio is not to assist, but to communicate effectively with others in joint work.

They believe that in the common work, the transmission of skills and knowledge is inseparable from the effective communication related to body information. As we often say, "Standing from the other side", if you don't look at things from the perspective of others, you can't resonate with them, and you can't understand each other's ideas.

Not only that, in daily conversations, in addition to speech, body language, eyes are also included, for example, if the Italian hands are tied up, they may not be able to properly express. Furthermore, physical activity is more important, such as skill learning. Most instructors adjust the position, posture, or direct guidance of the learner.

Therefore, in order to realize the sharing of visual viewpoints, technical guidance by remote manipulation has spawned a special wearable system called "Fusion".

Fusion can be divided into a controller side and a wearer side, the former using a commercially available head mounted device and a tactile feedback joystick. The latter is equipped with a 3-DOF machine eye and a pair of 6-degree-of-free humanoid robot arms in the backpack. In addition, the palm of the hand at the front of the arm can be removed to replace other connectors.

In this way, the instructor and the learner can have the same viewpoint, and the instructor can also drive the arm worn on the learner by means of remote manipulation to perform movement and technical guidance.

The study is also expected to be released on August 12 (local time) at the International Society "SIGGRAPH 2018" in Vancouver, Canada.
