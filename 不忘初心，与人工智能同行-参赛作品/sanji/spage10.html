In the future, AI can be used for 5G network analysis.

Many blogs and vendors' papers now cover 5G, ranging from new media transmissions to gigabit speeds to mobile devices. With seemingly unlimited bandwidth possibilities, the added value of moving to 5G is obvious, but we need to consider the impact of this type of network, the number of cellular sites and managed devices will increase dramatically, and today's network design can It is not clear what to deal with them.

Handling new levels of complexity and the possibility of operational failure

The true vision of 5G is to provide gigabit-speed mobile services and wireless fixed broadband, forming a single 5G network with new wireless access types and spectrum. This adds significant complexity to existing wireless networks. For example, a mobile provider may need to add a new client device (CPE) to deliver video media in the customer's home. CPE devices include wireless set-top boxes (or dongle), wireless routers, and wireless bridges that use these devices to forward UHF (eg, 30Ghz) signals from outside to the home. These wireless relay devices may become commonplace as the frequency of CSP adoption is higher. Due to the increasing use of spectrum, the design of the RAN must change. Currently, CSPs may already have 70,000 cellular sites running, but soon they will find themselves running hundreds of thousands of cellular sites. As the number of managed devices 10x-100x for new CPEs increases, making operations more complex, each device generates thousands of data. Now that these millions of new devices can fail countless times every day, it is clear that current operational practices and systems are unable to maintain the desired level of service, so it is difficult to retain existing customers or win new customers. .

What should the operator do?

As CSPs prepare for 5G, those who might succeed in the 5G space will focus on bringing advanced network operations and customer experience systems to the network. However, in the case of an increase in the number of network elements and connected devices, it is almost impossible to run a 5G network without the aid of AI analysis.

New consumer applications will drive a huge shift in the network. With the widespread use of augmented reality and virtual reality, consumer demand for these applications will further consume bandwidth and increase network variability. This will drive the development of higher-density computing and storage systems that are closer to consumers and at the edge of the network. A smaller, decentralized data center is needed to support the burst capacity of everything from the network to the service. The deployment of NFV and SDN and the virtualization applications of hardware-based systems that have previously spread across the country require specific analysis and proper management. Next-generation AI-based tools can be used to predict the impact of service failures and automatically trigger workflows to fix problems encountered by customers.

In addition, we have seen a big shift in customer expectations. Have you tried calling Google to support Gmail? You can't. From the beginning, many of our favorite content providers have developed customer self-service systems, coupled with advanced analytics and automation to create consistent services for customers at any time of the day. The CSP will be pushed in the same direction. 5G not only represents an important network transformation, but also represents the transformation point of customer participation and network operation.

To achieve this transformation, CSP needed an analytics framework to enhance the deployment of existing data pools by adding a rich set of analytics and application accelerators to extract and leverage the data stored in the data pool, adding missing data, and Quickly create analytics applications that solve critical business problems. Creating applications that provide fast, real-time access to data is critical to achieving a transition to 5G and true transitions.

Use AI's analysis to change the way you interact with your customers

Accessible data not only changes how CSPs operate the network, but also changes how they interact with customers. In today's more typical scenario, customer service, marketing, and network operations are separate departments that do not share data with each other. From a customer's perspective, this can lead to isolated interactions, and customers may have very different experiences in each department. When they want a basic connection to be fixed, the marketing department may be trying to market new features or services to customers. AI-driven analytics can share data from the network, allowing all interested departments to easily view customers as a whole with raw and processed data. Each department can see the complete data of the customer and then create department-specific applications as needed.

Next-generation network operations systems make it easy to analyze the trillions of pieces of data that servers, applications, radios, routers, switches, SIMs, IoT modules and CPE seamlessly generate. These analyses will be able to see small changes in normal mode and an indication of impending warnings. With this information, CSPs have the opportunity to categorize the data, prioritize workloads, and customers can use the system for self-service. In addition to predicting failures, the analysis will help the operations team identify systems that may be causing problems more quickly. By actively engaging with customers, many customers' calls and tickets can be avoided. By increasing uptime, higher quality services and better overall experience can attract higher levels of customer engagement.
