AWS three big AI solutions: image recognition, chat robot and voice conversion

COMPUTEX 2017 is a new special zone of InnoVEX, featuring innovative ecology, digital economy, Internet of Things (IoT) and artificial intelligence (AI). It brings together speakers from different countries to share the future development direction of this field at the exhibition forum.

Among them, Zane Moi, director of business development for Hong Kong, Macao and Taiwan at Amazon Cloud Computing Services (AWS), gave a speech on AI & Deep Learning at Amazon, explaining the main application prospects of AWS's AI and Deep Learning technologies in its business and consumer markets.

In business, AWS uses Kiva robots to create large-scale warehousing automation; in products, Amazon Echo combines with AI voice assistant Alexa to make life easier for consumers at home, and Primeair makes intelligent delivery services; in services, Amazon Go unmanned stores use in-depth learning calculus technology to record consumer purchases. Create Just Walk Out Technology to create an unprecedented shopping experience for consumers.

In recent years, more efforts have been made to use AI and in-depth learning to build its intellectual infrastructure. Moi facilitates the sharing of three AWS AI service solutions, image recognition system Amazon Rekognition, chat robot Lex and text-to-speech program Polly.

Rekognition recognizes what is made up of images in a photograph, and effectively recognizes and memorizes faces; for example, the American television channel C-Space uses the program to automatically recognize speakers'faces in television programs. By collecting more than 97,000 portraits, Rekognition eventually succeeded in identifying the same designated person in eight different program clips on C-Space, allowing users to know who the speaker is and to learn more about what shows he has appeared in.

Amazon Lex and Amazon Polly are chat robot services and word-to-speech programs, both using natural language understanding (NLU) and automatic speech recognition (ASR) technology. Lex can be widely used to answer consumer questions, applications and other chat robots, but also can be used to enhance the productivity of enterprises as automated robots and IoT robots. Polly, on the other hand, can translate words into vivid language, understand written implicatures through AI technology, and translate them precisely into spoken sentences.

From home warehouse management, home smart life, to giving consumers a disruptive and innovative shopping experience, to three solutions, the AWS vision is to make machines with AI and in-depth learning intelligence deep into everyday life.
