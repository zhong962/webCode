Jurgen: AI has long been conscious of where the industry will go in the next ten years

JU rgen Schmidhuber is absolutely a strange name in China's artificial intelligence industry. Even for AI practitioners all over the world, he is also a very low-key technology elder. Living and working in Switzerland, although far from the two noisy centers of the United States and China, the LSTM (Long Short-Term Memory) time recurrent neural network invented by Jurgen has been popular in artificial intelligence research all over the world.

Jurgen is currently director of science at the Swiss Artificial Intelligence Laboratory (IDSIA) and teaches at the University of Lugano and the School of Applied Sciences and Arts in Southern Switzerland. As the first Fellow of the International Association of Computational Languages (ACL), Yu Ergen has made great achievements in machine learning, computer vision and natural language processing. He co-founded LSTM with Sepp Hochreiter and laid the foundation for the success of recurrent neural networks in ORC, speech recognition and other applications today.

For the industry's long-awaited "general AI" (AGI), Yu Ergen believes that AI can usually connect AI with human characteristics such as consciousness, emotion, knowledge and consciousness, and one of the most critical issues is that it is not subject to designer constraints. General AI systems are moving towards reality, which means that some AI systems are already conscious. Jurgen's study found that agents create a signal to compress all the behaviors around them. The most effective way is to find a signal or a small subnetwork to replace themselves, including all the actions, the results and so on. It can be aware of itself, present itself and self-feedback, and compress observation history in unsupervised model networks.

For a long time, why do people think artificial intelligence is unconsciousness? Juergen explained that this is because the consciousness system of the agent is still very simple, with only a few hundred connections. This is very few compared with the human consciousness system with billions of connections. However, this is only a quantitative difference, and the quality is the same.

"If we build a system in the future that has more connections than the human brain, consciousness will be a byproduct of data compression, and there's no need to write some everyday awareness to the agent, because the agent will become aware with the data compression." Jurgen said.

AlphaGo is not universal AI, because it does not have complete consciousness and emotion

Reporter: how do you think of AlphaGo and Kirk's human-machine war? Some people say that AlphaGo has reached the level of development of general AI (AGI). Do you agree with it?

Jurgen: first of all, I am very interested in this competition. Because I've been working on General Artificial Intelligence since 1987 and published my first article on General Artificial Intelligence in the same year. That's an article about machine learning, especially how to learn, so that humans have no limitations in learning, but it's not just about how to enhance brain areas that control behavior. I studied for a long time, and although the results were not obvious, my student Cyp Hockles contributed a lot to Deep Mind. At the same time, one of the founders of Deep Mind, Shane Legge, also studied superintelligence in my lab. In fact, when Mustafa Suleiman, one of the founders of DeepMind, founded the company, he also studied biological neuroscience. DeepMind is closely related to AGI, and I have deep roots in AGI.

But I don't think Alpha Go is showing "general AI" in this man-machine war. In 1990s, we already had a basic conscious learning system. At that time, I proposed that the general learning system should contain two modules. One is a cyclic network controller, in which machines learn to receive data and then convert it into action. This network can learn to convert all these inputs into action sequences in time. Since 1990, our agents have been trying to do the same thing, using an additional loop network (an unsupervised module) to predict what will happen.

I don't think AlphaGo (at least for the time being) is a general AI because it's only used to deal with specific problems, and it doesn't need to have the full cognitive power of humans, or even the sensory cognitive power of humans, but it just looks like wisdom. In Go, Alpha Go only needs to analyze the board with nineteen equidistant, perpendicularly crossed parallel lines, to decide where to play next, and to focus on this matter, regardless of the environment. It doesn't need to think about the history of Go, the stories and emotions behind it, which is different from real life. In real life, human contact with the world around countless, even so, human can not see all the meaning behind. The human brain has so much to do that you need to remember what you said and did last time, but Alpha Go doesn't need to, and doesn't need to give feedback. So, I don't think Alphago, which is used in Go games, is really "general AI" in our sense.

AI companies all over the world are using LSTM, but this is only a small part of AI.

Reporter: LSTM (Long Short-Term Memory) neural network is very popular in artificial intelligence research, and is used in many companies and areas of research and development, can you give some examples? As a founder, will you update and improve LSTM in the future?

Jurgen: as far as I know, LSTM is used by Google to track millions of Android phones. At the same time, Google has introduced new technologies, and Google translation has made great progress. Previously, when Google translation was not perfect, Chinese people would joke about the results of translation, such as in the process of Chinese-English translation, because the system is not perfect, it will produce ridiculous translation results. Google translation has changed a lot now, because it's built on LSTM and has made great progress.

At the same time, LSTM can also be used for composing music. For example, when you listen to music, if you hear a good piece of music, you can use the LSTM network to find the entire piece, and you can also use it to create more tracks. LSTM can also be used for subtitling movies, predicting what people want to say, predicting the stock market, maintaining machines (signaling before machines are scrapped, letting people replace them with new ones) and so on.

These examples are very effective and cost effective. It can be seen that LSTM has a wide range of applications. However, this is not the general purpose of AI. At present, AI can only recognize speech, language and so on. However, unlike the process of infant learning, AI does not interact with the world around it, establish three views, and learn how to interact with the world to achieve its goals. It is the purpose of general AI to learn for the purpose and do not require teachers' guidance. But I'm very happy to see that so many people around the world are using LSTM, but it's only a small part of real artificial intelligence. What we are more interested in is how to implement AGI and achieve real AI.

LSTM is the best way to solve the AI problem at present. Maybe we will find a better way in the future. After all, technology always has room for improvement. In the future, artificial intelligence is expected to automatically search for buildings. In 2009, one of my Ph.D. students mentioned in his paper that it is possible that artificial intelligence could evolve automatically in the future. And according to different applications, different improvements can be found in the future.
