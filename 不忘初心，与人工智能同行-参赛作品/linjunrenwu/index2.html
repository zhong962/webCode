20 Chinese AI Leaders

Leading companies in China's science and technology sector have been investing heavily in artificial intelligence recently, and Chinese AI technology has really gained a foothold in the world's top AI sector.The number of research reports published by China's academic research institutions exceeds that of leading AI countries such as the us and the UK.In areas such as health care, China may already be ahead of the United States in applying AI technology to social problems.

The following executives, entrepreneurs, professors or researchers are leading China's most important and cutting-edge AI companies and research LABS.Their professionalism and achievements are widely recognized and respected both within and outside the industry.

Li Kaifu

President &CEO of innovation works, former global vice President of Google and President of greater China

Li Kaifu is an active leader in the international arena of scientific and technological innovation. He holds an undergraduate degree in computer science from Columbia University and a PhD in computer science from Carnegie Mellon university.The innovation works he has invested in.

Lu Qi

President and chief operating officer of baidu group

Before joining baidu, Mr. Lu was recruited by Steve Ballmer to become executive vice President of Microsoft's app and services division.Mr. Lu began his career at IBM research, then joined yahoo and rose to executive vice President of search and advertising.Lu got his bachelor's degree in computer science at fudan university, and then got a PhD at Carnegie Mellon university at the invitation of professor Edmund m. Clarke.

Wang Haifeng

Vice President of baidu and general director of AI department

Wang Haifeng's technical specialty is natural language processing and mechanical translation;He has published over 100 academic reports on AI.Wang has integrated his professional knowledge and insights into baidu's natural language processing, computer vision, speech recognition, knowledge charts, personalized advice and deep learning projects.As an alumnus, wang haifeng is also a visiting professor at Harbin Institute of Technology.

Zhang Tong

Director of tencent AI Lab

Zhang Tong was a professor at rutgers university in the United States, and later worked at IBM and yahoo. Before coming to tencent, he was head of big data lab at baidu.Currently, zhangtong leads a team of tencent AI, consisting of 50 scientists and 250 engineers, in the fields of machine learning, computer vision, speech recognition and natural language processing.He has tried to apply ai to video games, social media, content and platform tools and a variety of mobile applications.

Zhou Jingren

Chief scientist of aliyun

Founded in 2009, aliyun is now alibaba's fastest-growing division.Like Amazon Web Services, aliyun has the computing power to handle millions of online shopping transactions for alibaba.Zhou jingren leads research on big data and artificial intelligence at alibaba's cloud data science and technology institute.He USES alibaba's AI technology in natural language, image and video processing, and in large machine learning.

Ye Jieping

Vice President of didi research institute

Didi dache collects more than 50 terabytes of real-time data and more than 9 billion rides a day.As the brain of didi chuxing, didi research institute is a machine learning research center to predict customer demand for didi chuxing, reduce peak impact and develop self-driving technology.Ye jieping, who leads didi's big data team, had an undergraduate degree in mathematics at fudan university and a PhD in computer science at the university of Minnesota.He is also a tenured professor at the university of Michigan and an internationally renowned expert in machine learning.

Lin Yuanqing

Director of baidu research institute and director of baidu deep learning laboratory

Lin manages major baidu research LABS, including silicon valley AI lab (SVAIL), big data lab (BDL), augmented reality lab (ARL) and deep learning lab (IDL).The national engineering laboratory for deep learning technology and application, funded by the Chinese government and co-sponsored by tsinghua university and Beijing university of aeronautics and astronautics.

Zhu Pinpin

Xiao I robot founder, President and CTO

Xiao I is a leading provider of intelligent robot platform and architecture in China, providing experience and support for most robots and virtual assistants across the country.Founded in Shanghai in 2001, xiao I's technology and products are used by hundreds of smes, government agencies and more than 500 million users.Zhu used numerous patents to promote the technical advantages of small I in the dialogue interface, including "chatbot system" and "SMS robot system".Zhu, who is not only the President of xiao I, but also a doctor of science of the Chinese academy of sciences.

Xu Wei

Distinguished scientist of baidu and senior director of baidu deep learning laboratory

Baidu has many top scientists, but Xu wei is the only one to be awarded the title of "outstanding scientist".Xu wei's contribution to PaddlePaddle's deep learning toolkit and professional skills have made him highly respected within baidu.In more than three years of development.

Min Wanli

Alibaba digital science director and chief scientist of ali cloud artificial intelligence

Min led the development of aliyun artificial intelligence system, known as xiaoai.Xiaoai is a tool for alibaba's internal deployment of services for its flagship business, including customer service and traffic prediction.Min also used machine learning to predict the winner of " I am a singer".

Jing Kun

General manager of baidu Duer department

Duer is an AI voice platform created by baidu in response to apple's Siri, amazon's Alexa, Microsoft's Cortana and Google's Google assistant.Before baidu, jing kun was the director of research and development at Microsoft and developed the chatbot Xiaoice, widely popular on tencent's WeChat and sina weibo.Xiaoice, the most influential app on weibo, has 20 million registered users, who use it an average of 60 times a month.

Yu Dong

Deputy director of tencent AI Lab

Yu is in charge of tencent's AI lab in Seattle, which focuses on speech recognition and natural language understanding.Before joining tencent, yu served as the principal researcher of Microsoft research's speech and dialogue group, visiting professor of zhejiang university and China university of science and technology, and visiting researcher of Shanghai jiaotong university.Yu dong graduated from the department of electrical engineering of zhejiang university.

Adam Coates

Director of baidu silicon valley AI lab

Coates has bachelor's, master's, and doctoral degrees in computer science at Stanford university, working on computer vision for autonomous vehicles, deep learning for speech recognition, and machine learning for helicopters.He worked in baidu's DeepSpeech department, developing the speech recognition and translation engine that can speak mandarin like a native Chinese.He was also in charge of DeepVoice, a synthetic engine that turns text into speech.

Yu kai

Founder &CEO of Horizon Robotics

Yu kai, a former director of baidu's deep learning institute, is currently working on developing horizon robotics, a start-up he founded.Horizon got money from prominent investors including yuri milner and sequoia capital.Yu's idea was to turn horizon into "android in robots," an AI system that works with all smart devices.Unlike the cloud technology dominated by other Chinese tech giants, horizon focuses on applying AI systems to all hardware.

Wang Jin

Former senior vice President of baidu

During his tenure, wang managed over 5,000 engineers from various departments, many of which were founded by him, including mobile cloud business division, infrastructure (cloud computing) division, big data division, network security division, baidu research institute and baidu self-drive department.After Mr. Ng's resignation, Mr. Wang left baidu and founded a self-driving startup.Inside and outside the industry, wang jin's leadership and contribution to baidu's self-driving technology is widely recognized.

Zhang Bo

Professor, department of computer science and technology, qinghua university

Professor zhang's research interests include artificial intelligence, machine learning, pattern recognition, knowledge engineering and robotics.His outstanding academic achievements include machine learning algorithms for robotic tasks and mobile planning, probabilistic logical neural networks (PLN), image retrieval and classification, and web site structure mining.

Wu Hua

Chairman of baidu technical committee

During his seven years at baidu, wu led a series of key technological breakthroughs in natural language processing (NLP), dialogue systems and neural machine translation (NMT), successfully attracting hundreds of millions of users to baidu's translation products.The New York times praised her research on multitasking as "groundbreaking."

Li Wei

Dean and professor of computer science, Beijing university of aeronautics and astronautics, academician of Chinese academy of sciences

Li wei has systematically solved the semantic problems of practical parallel programming languages, especially the parallel and communication structures, and made a founding contribution to the translation of parallel programming languages correctly.Professor li wei's technical contributions to artificial intelligence and network computing have won him numerous honors and awards.

Cha Hongbin

Associate dean, professor and tutor of school of science and technology, Beijing University

Cha has worked with Microsoft research Asia and other leading AI organizations or individuals in Asia."China hopes to surpass western countries, including the United States, in this area by investing heavily and rapidly in the AI industry," cha once said.

Chen Yunji

Professor, institute of computing technology, Chinese academy of sciences

Chen entered college at 14 and took his PhD at 24 at lightning speed.He is now the chief designer of the loongson 3C, a microprocessing chip that reduces the power required for computers to recognise objects and translate languages.He also developed Cambrico, a processor chip that simulates human nerve cells to facilitate deep learning.The team, led by Chen yunji and his younger brother Chen tianshi, are also the youngest professors at the Chinese academy of sciences.
