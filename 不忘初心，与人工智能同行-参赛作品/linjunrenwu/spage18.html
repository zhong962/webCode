The father of convolutional neural networks: artificial intelligence, the next step to learn common sense from the video

As a big cow in the field of deep learning, YannLeCun has driven Facebook's artificial intelligence projects to advance rapidly in recent years, and its momentum has not fallen behind. Today Facebook's face recognition technology DeepFace has been able to identify more than 400 million faces, and YannLeCun is not satisfied with this. He believes that machine vision should not be satisfied with the recognition from the picture. The next step should be to learn common sense from the video. ".

Five years ago, researchers made great breakthroughs in the accuracy of image recognition. The technology behind this, the artificial neural network, has contributed to the recent boom in artificial intelligence. It allows Google and Faceboo to let you use search in your own albums, and to make a batch of applications that use facial recognition come out.

Facebook's AI research team leader and professor at New York University pioneered the use of neural networks in machine vision. He believes that there is still a lot of room for development in this field, and advances in this area may lead to a software system that understands common sense.

Q: What is the current level of machine vision?

YannLeCun: If your image has a significant body, just mark the subject's category. If there is enough data—about a thousand images in each category—we can identify something quite specific: a car of a certain brand, a particular species of plant, a dog of a particular lineage. We can also identify more abstract things like landscapes, sunsets, weddings or birthday parties. It was not clear five years ago that this problem can be solved. (Now we have already overcome it) But this is not to say that the visual problem has been solved.

Q: What are the key issues that have not been resolved yet?

YannLeCun: People have been working hard for many years to automatically generate captions and annotations for images and videos. Some of the seemingly impressive ways have been born before, but in reality they are not as reliable. Their scope of application is greatly limited by how they are trained. For most systems, if you provide them with images that contain other kinds of objects or are in an unconventional situation, their recognition status will be terrible. They don't have common sense.

Q: What is the connection between vision and common sense?

YannLeCun: It depends on who you are talking to – even inside Facebook, everyone has different opinions. You can communicate with a set of intelligent systems in only one language. The problem is that language is a low-bandwidth information channel. People can pass a lot of information through the language because they have a lot of background knowledge to explain this information.

Others believe that the only way to provide enough information to an AI system is to base its visual perception, which is much more informative than the language. At this time, if you tell the machine "This is a smart phone", "This is a road roller", "There are some things you can push but those that don't work", then the machine may learn some basic knowledge about how the world works.

We really want the machine to get a lot of facts that reflect the laws of the real world by watching videos or other ways. This will eventually give them common sense. The learning process of juvenile animals and the first few months of life is very interesting – they have an amazing understanding of the world in a short period of time just by observation. Today's machines are still easily fooled by various means, because they know too little about the world.

Q: What progress has been made in making intelligent systems learn through observation?

YannLeCun: A learning system should be predictable in the future, and we are very interested in this idea. You show a few frames of video to the learning system, and then it predicts what will happen next. If we can train a system that can do this kind of work, then I think we have built the basic technology of an unsupervised learning system. I think this will be a node, creating possibilities for many interesting things. Its scope of application is not limited to machine vision - this is an important part of our continuous advancement in the AI ​​field.
