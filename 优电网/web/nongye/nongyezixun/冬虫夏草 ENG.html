Experts and Scholars: Integrating Cordyceps Resources to Create Common Brands and Standards

Cordyceps sinensis, also known as cordyceps, Cordyceps sinensis and Cordyceps sinensis, is one of the most precious medicines in our country. Its market price is very high, and its health care effect is very strong. It can effectively regulate the immunity of human body, as well as resist tumors and alleviate fatigue. It has the reputation of "golden grass". At the Summit Forum of Cordyceps sinensis held in Yushu, Qinghai on the 21st, experts and scholars concerned expressed that Cordyceps sinensis resources should be integrated to create a common brand and standard.

The output of Cordyceps sinensis in Qinghai accounts for 60% of China's total output, and the output of Cordyceps sinensis in Yushu area accounts for 80% of Qinghai's total output. Cordyceps sinensis has the characteristics of large size, good quality, good color and long cephalosporin this area. Zaduo County in Yushu prefecture has the reputation of "the first county of Cordyceps sinensis in China".
  Liu Xingzhong, a researcher at the Institute of Microbiology, Chinese Academy of Sciences, said that the image of Cordyceps sinensis should be established from the source, its efficacy components should be studied and an overall development plan should be issued to integrate China's resources and form a common brand and standard.


Cordyceps sinensis


Lin Wenfei, director of the Large Wild Fungi Specimens Laboratory of the College of Life Sciences, Zhejiang University, believes that Chinese Cordyceps sinensis has a thousand-year history of edible and medicinal homology. With the increasing demand, the market should be correctly guided by the latest research results.
  Chen Shijiang, director of Cordyceps sinensis research institute, Chongqing Academy of Traditional Chinese Medicine, admitted that Cordyceps sinensis pharmacology research has formed a system, there is no doubt that heavy metals should be studied in depth, and can not be generalized.
&emsp: "The arsenic contained in Cordyceps sinensis is mainly organic arsenic, which is harmless to human body like kelp." Li Yuling, director of Cordyceps sinensis research department, Academy of Animal Husbandry and Veterinary Sciences, Qinghai University, said that in recent years, Cordyceps sinensis has been paying more and more attention. We should solve the coordinated development of ecology and resources, form a management mechanism of Cordyceps sinensis, and give full play to the efficiency of production areas through the advantages of germplasm resources.
Xie Fang, director of the Institute of Species and Seedling Engineering of Lanzhou Jiaotong University, said that the inorganic proportion of Cordyceps sinensis arsenic was small, but there was no authoritative systematic report. Scientific and technological innovation and research should be strengthened, and the transformation of Cordyceps sinensis industry from collection to cultivation should be guided.
  Lin Pengcheng, Dean of the College of Chemistry and Life Sciences, Qinghai University for Nationalities, said that the role of small components in a medicinal material could not be denied. Current research on Cordyceps sinensis should use modern separation methods to promote the development of the whole industry.
Zha Xicaiji, chairman of Qinghai Yushu Sanjiangyuan Science and Technology Co., Ltd., said that he hoped that Cordyceps sinensis could develop sustainably and transform from medicinal materials to medicines and health care products in the future, and build a diversified product and market with the help of the latest research results.
