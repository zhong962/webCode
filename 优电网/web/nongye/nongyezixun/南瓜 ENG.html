Molecular mechanism of salt tolerance difference between pumpkin and cucumber

Recently, the Journal of Experimental Botany published online research papers on teamwork between Professor Bie Zhilong of Central China Agricultural University and Professor Sergey Shabala of the University of Tasmania in Australia. The important molecular physiological mechanism of salt tolerance in pumpkin was analyzed. The main reason was that RBOHD produced hydrogen peroxide (H2O2) signal in root tips of pumpkin under salt stress, which regulated potassium uptake through plasma membrane H+-ATPase, and then affected the vitality and salt tolerance of root tip cells of pumpkin, but the regulation of potassium uptake in cucumber was not obvious.


Pumpkin is more salt-tolerant than cucumber


  the salt tolerance of pumpkin is stronger than that of cucumber, which is an important rootstock of cucumber. Grafting cucumber onto the rootstock of pumpkin can significantly improve the salt tolerance. However, the molecular physiological mechanism of salt tolerance differences between pumpkin and cucumber has not been clear. As an important active oxygen signal molecule, hydrogen peroxide participates in many plant growth, development and resistance regulation processes.
In this study, cell viability staining, non-damage ion selective microelectrodes (MIFE), potassium content determination, and fluorescence imaging of hydrogen peroxide were used to study the salt tolerance of pumpkin, which was stronger than that of cucumber, and the root tips of pumpkin had higher potassium absorption rate, potassium content and hydrogen peroxide level. The transcriptome study showed that 85% of the differential genes in the root tip of pumpkin were significantly up-regulated, while only 52% of the differential genes in cucumber were significantly up-regulated. NADPH oxidase, 14-3-3 protein, plasma membrane H +-ATPase and potassium transport related genes RBOHD, GRF12, AHA1 and HAK5 were significantly up-regulated under salt stress in pumpkin, but not in cucumber.
emsp; pretreatment of roots with DPI, an inhibitor of NADPH oxidase, showed that the formation of hydrogen peroxide was blocked, the expression of GRF12, AHA1, HAK5, the activity of plasma membrane H +-ATPase and the rate of potassium uptake were significantly decreased, while that of exogenous hydrogen peroxide treatment was the opposite. Furthermore, CRISPR/Cas9 gene editing and Arabidopsis thaliana transgenic methods were used to verify the molecular physiological mechanism of hydrogen peroxide signaling produced by pumpkin RBOHD regulating potassium uptake and salt tolerance through plasma membrane H +-ATPase.
