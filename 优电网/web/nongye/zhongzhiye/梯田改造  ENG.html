FARMING\ Terrace transformation

Terrace

There are terraces in many parts of China, which are distributed up the steep slopes, like steps built for giants to climb to the sky. Terraced fields cut into the hillside for planting crops, which is one of the most amazing ways for human beings to transform the surface morphology.

 Terraces are fields with strip stepped or wavy sections built along contour lines on hilly slopes. It is an effective measure to control soil and water loss on sloping farmland, and has a significant effect on water storage, soil conservation and yield increase. Terraces have better ventilation and light transmission conditions, which are conducive to crop growth and nutrient accumulation. Horizontal terraces, sloping terraces and compound terraces are available according to the gradient of the field surface. The width of terrace depends on the size of slope, thickness of soil layer, tillage mode, labor and economic conditions, and unified planning with irrigation and drainage system and traffic road. When terraces are built, topsoil should be retained. After terraces are built, agricultural tillage measures such as deep ploughing, increasing organic fertilizer and planting appropriate pioneer crops should be taken to accelerate soil maturation and improve soil fertility.

Terrace history

 China had terraced fields as early as the Qin and Han Dynasties. In order to solve the food problem, the migrant farmers built terraces and conserved water resources with dykes and dams, which made it possible to grow rice in large areas in hilly areas and solved the local food problem. Question. However, terrace cultivation consumes much more manpower than plain cultivation, and has no advantage in yield. Moreover, it destroys vegetation seriously in Hilly areas, so this cultivation method has been gradually eliminated, and now it is only for tourist attractions.

Terrace distribution

  terraces in China are mainly distributed in the mountains of the south of the Yangtze River, and most of them are in Guangxi and Yunnan. This is because there are more rainwater and mountains in these areas. Terraces are built on mountains. Among them, Ailao Mountain in Yunnan and Longsheng Longji Terraces in Guangxi are more famous.
  Hani Terrace: Yunnan is mountainous and terraced. Ailaoshan Hani Terrace is the representative work of Yunnan Terrace and is known as "the most beautiful mountain sculpture in China".
  Longsheng Longji Terrace Scenic Area is a famous scenic spot in Guangxi. It was built in the Yuan Dynasty and has a history of nearly 700 years. She is the crystallization of diligence and wisdom of the Zhuang and Yao nationalities. Longji Terrace Scenic Area is mainly divided into Ping'an Zhuangzhai Terrace and Jinkeng Hongyao Terrace. There are two famous landscapes of "seven stars accompanying the moon" and "nine dragons and five tigers" in the terraces of Ping'an Zhuang Village. The landscapes of Jinkeng Hongyao Terrace are beautiful and elegant. There are three famous landscapes of "large boundaries thousand-storey ladder", "Xishan Shaolai" and "Jinfoding" in the terraces of Jinkeng Hongyao, which are magnificent and straight up to the clouds. The whole Longji Terrace Scenic Area has beautiful mountains, clear waters, waterfalls, silver belts in spring, green waves in summer raceway, golden pagodas in autumn, dragons and water in winter, and each season has its own charm. The Zhuang and Yao nationalities here have lived in the Qingwamulou for generations. They are simple and kind.
