Power for all

1.4 billion people are electrified by the whole people. How does China do it?

        Published: 2019-07-08         big midsmall

　　As of the end of 2018

　　When the world’s power generation growth rate is only 3.7%

　　China leads the world with a rapid growth rate of 8.4%

　　The annual power generation reached 711.1 billion kWh

　　Almost as "the power of oneself"

　　Produced more than a quarter of the world's electricity

　　Average power generated every 2 seconds

　　It is enough to satisfy a Chinese

　　Life needs for a lifetime

　　▼The above average life expectancy of Chinese people is 76 years old. The per capita electricity consumption is based on 2018 data. The following picture shows the top 1010 power generation in the world. Cartography @郑伯容/星球研究所

　　Not only that

　　Looking at 233 countries and regions around the world

　　China is still the first

　　Also the only one

　　With a huge population of nearly 1.4 billion

　　Still able to do the nationwide powering

　　▼Shanghai night satellite map, brightly lit city, picture source @NASA

　　How is China doing it?

　　I, 70.4%

　　In this 711.1 billion kWh of electricity

　　70.4% from thermal power generation

　　Can be described as the majority of the country's electricity

　　Even straw, garbage, etc.

　　▼Guangzhou China Resources Thermal Power Plant, built by Linshui, photographer @陈国亨

　　The ratio is almost 90%

　　More than 5,800 large and small coal mines nationwide

　　An annual output of about 3.68 billion tons of raw coal

　　More than half of the output

　　Will be shipped to these power plants to burn

　　To answer this question

　　It’s better to shift your gaze to

　　Between Shanxi Datong and Hebei Qinhuangdao

　　It is connected to a famous railway.

　　It does not exceed 0.5% of the national railway mileage.

　　Completed nearly 20% of the coal traffic of the whole railway

　　Heading to the shores of the Bohai Sea, thousands of miles away

　　The length of a single train is nearly 4,000 meters

　　Equivalent to 10-20 trains connected by high-speed trains

　　After the coal is transported to Qinhuangdao Port

　　Can pass the lower cost shipping

　　Shipped to the east and southeast coast

　　however

　　Rapid growth with demand for electricity

　　The mighty "Western Coal East Transportation" and "Northern Coal South Transportation"

　　Still not a once and for all solution

　　Around the main electrical load center

　　Often small and medium-sized thermal power plants

　　But when producing the same amount of electricity

　　Coal consumption is 30-50% higher than that of large power plants

　　In a time when technology and economy are still underdeveloped

　　Smoke from these small and medium thermal power plants

　　Air pollutants such as sulfur dioxide and nitrogen oxides

　　It is also difficult to get uniform and efficient processing

　　So since the 1960s

　　Near coal mines, transit ports

　　Many large thermal power plants are beginning to rise

　　▼Shanxi Gujiao Power Plant, adjacent to the coal mine mouth, also known as Hangkou Power Station, photographer @陈剑峰

　　For example, the Tokto Power Plant in Hohhot, Inner Mongolia

　　Only 50km from the Zhungeer large coal field

　　Installed capacity reached 6.72 million kilowatts

　　Ranked first in the world coal-fired power plant

　　Construction of large pits and port power plants

　　Can greatly reduce the pressure of coal transportation

　　Improve coal combustion efficiency and uniformly control emissions

　　But between the power plant and the load center

　　Sometimes thousands of kilometers apart

　　How can this be solved?

　　The answer is actually very simple

　　Is power transmission

　　But it’s not easy to achieve

　　After all, under such a long distance of transmission

　　The impedance of the line can not be ignored

　　People can only reduce the transmission current as much as possible

　　To minimize line loss

　　This means

　　When the transmission power is constant

　　While ensuring economy

　　Must increase the transmission voltage as much as possible

　　▼The loss Q in transmission can be calculated by the formula Q=I2Rt. When the resistance R cannot be ignored, the smaller the current I is, the smaller the loss is. The calculation formula of the transmission power is P=I×U, so when the power P is rated, In order to reduce the current I, the voltage U must be raised; the picture below shows the line tower in the mountain of Kangding, and the photographer @李珩

　　1954

　　220 kV high voltage transmission line

　　Transmission distance 369 km

　　65 years passed

　　From high pressure to ultra high pressure

　　From ultra high pressure to extra high pressure

　　Long-distance transmission technology is advancing by leaps and bounds

　　The highest voltage level has been reached

　　AC 1000 kV and DC ±1100 kV

　　The transmission distance of a single line is more than 3,000 kilometers.

　　Equivalent to the straight line distance from Urumqi to Nanjing

　　No. 1 in the world

　　▼ For AC transmission, 35-220 kV is called high voltage, 330-1000 kV is ultra-high voltage, 1000 kV and above is UHV; for DC transmission, ±400-±660 kV is ultra-high voltage, ±800 kV And above is UHV. The picture below is from Jiuquan to Hunan ±800 kV UHV DC transmission line, photographer @刘忠文
