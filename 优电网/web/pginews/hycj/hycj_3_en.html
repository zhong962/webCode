Three Gorges Hydropower Station

The Three Gorges Hydropower Project, the Yangtze River Three Gorges Water Conservancy Project, is also known as the Three Gorges Project. The Xiling Gorge section of the Yangtze River and the Gezhouba Hydropower Station in the lower reaches of Yichang City, Hubei Province, China constitute a cascade hydropower station.

The Three Gorges Hydropower Station is the largest hydropower station in the world and the largest construction project ever undertaken in China. The problems such as immigration relocation and environment caused by it have caused it to be accompanied by huge controversies from the moment of preparation. There are more than ten functions of the Three Gorges Hydropower Station, shipping, power generation, planting, etc. The Three Gorges Hydropower Station was approved by the National People's Congress of China in 1992 and officially started construction in 1994. Water storage and power generation began on the afternoon of June 1, 2003, and was completed in 2009.

The unit equipment is mainly composed of VOITH, GE, SIEMENS, VGS consortium, ALSTOM, and ABB, Switzerland. provide. When they signed the supply agreement, they all promised to transfer the relevant technology to China's domestic motor manufacturing enterprises without compensation. The power transmission and transformation system of the Three Gorges Hydropower Station is under construction and management by the State Grid Corporation of China. It is estimated that a total of 15 500 kV high-voltage transmission lines will be installed to connect to the regional power grids.

The dam of the Three Gorges Hydropower Station has an elevation of 185 meters, a water storage elevation of 175 meters, a reservoir length of 2,335 meters, a static investment of 135.266 billion yuan, and installation of 32 hydropower units with a single unit capacity of 700,000 kilowatts. The last hydropower unit of the Three Gorges Power Station was put into operation on July 4, 2012. This means that the Three Gorges Hydropower Station with an installed capacity of 22.4 million kilowatts has become the world's largest hydropower station and clean energy production base on July 4, 2012. . At 8:25:21 on December 21, 2018, the Three Gorges Project produced a total of 100 billion kWh of green energy under the premise of giving full play to the huge comprehensive benefits of flood control, shipping and water resources utilization.

Construction scale

The Three Gorges Dam is a concrete gravity dam. The dam is 2,335 meters long, 115 meters wide at the bottom, 40 meters wide at the top, 185 meters high, and 175 meters in normal water storage. The dam body can withstand the catastrophic floods once in a million years, and the maximum discharge flow can reach 100,000 cubic meters per second. The earth and stone excavation volume of the whole project is about 134 million cubic meters, the concrete pouring amount is about 28 million cubic meters, and the steel consumption is 593,000 tons. The total length of the reservoir is more than 600 kilometers, the average width of the water surface is 1.1 kilometers, the total area is 1084 square kilometers, and the total storage capacity is 39.3 billion cubic meters, of which the flood control capacity is 22.15 billion cubic meters, and the adjustment capacity is seasonally regulated.

The units of the Three Gorges Hydropower Station are arranged on the rear side of the dam. A total of 32 sets of 700,000 kW hydro-generator units are installed, including 14 on the left bank, 12 on the right bank, 6 underground, and 2 50,000-kilowatt power units. The installed capacity is 22.5 million kilowatts, far exceeding the Itaipu hydropower station in Brazil, which ranks second in the world.

The initial planning of the Three Gorges Power Station is 26 units of 700,000 kilowatts, that is, the installed capacity is 18.2 million kilowatts, and the annual power generation is 84.7 billion degrees. Later, an underground power station was built in the "Bai Shi Jian" mountain in the right bank dam, and six 700,000 kW hydroelectric generators were built. Together with the two 50,000-kilowatt power stations of the Three Gorges Power Station, the total installed capacity reached 22.5 million kilowatts.

China Yangtze River Three Gorges Corporation announced on the 1st that as of 24:00 on December 31, 2014, the Three Gorges Power Station generated an annual power generation of 98.8 billion kWh, creating a new world record for the annual power generation of single hydropower stations, and for the first time in the world. Hydropower station with the highest annual power generation.

The cumulative power generation of the Three Gorges Power Station is 98.8 billion kWh, which is equivalent to reducing the consumption of raw coal by more than 49 million tons and reducing carbon dioxide emissions by nearly 100 million tons. If the contribution of electricity per kWh to GDP is calculated at 10 yuan, the clean electric energy emitted by the Three Gorges Power Station in the whole year is equivalent to creating nearly one trillion yuan of wealth for the country. This has also injected a strong impetus into the country's "steady growth, restructuring, and benefiting people's livelihood."

The main function

The Three Gorges Project has three major benefits, namely flood control, power generation and shipping. Flood control is considered to be the core benefit of the Three Gorges Project. /p>

Flood control

Historically, the upper reaches of the Yangtze River and its multiple tributaries frequently flooded. During each major flood, the Jingzhou section of the Yangtze River (Jingjiang) below Yichang should adopt flood diversion measures to inundate villages and farmland to ensure the safety of Wuhan. After the completion of the Three Gorges Project, the capacity of the huge storage capacity provided by the huge storage capacity will enable the downstream Jingjiang area to withstand the catastrophic floods in the past 100 years, and also contribute to the treatment of the Dongting Lake and the comprehensive repair of the Jingjiang dike.

Power generation

The economic benefits of the Three Gorges Project are mainly reflected in power generation. The project is a giant power point in the middle line of China's West-East Power Transmission Project. The power generated will be sold to Hubei Province, Henan Province, Hunan Province, Jiangxi Province and Chongqing City of Huazhong Power Grid, and Shanghai, Jiangsu Province of East China Power Grid. Zhejiang Province, Anhui Province, and Guangdong Province of China Southern Power Grid can alleviate the tight supply of electricity in China.

As of the end of 2012, the accumulated power generation of the Three Gorges Power Station has reached 629.14 billion kWh over the years, equivalent to 496 million tons of carbon dioxide emission reduction and 5.95 million tons of sulfur dioxide emission reduction, making a positive contribution to energy conservation and emission reduction.

At 8:25:21 on December 21, 2018, the Three Gorges Project produced a total of 100 billion kWh of green energy under the premise of giving full play to the huge comprehensive benefits of flood control, shipping and water resources utilization. According to statistics, 100 billion kWh of green electricity is equivalent to saving 0.319 billion tons of standard coal and reducing carbon dioxide emissions by 85.8 billion tons. If the calculation of 12 yuan GDP per kWh of electricity, 100 billion kWh of electricity can support China's 1.2 trillion yuan of GDP

Shipping

Before the Three Gorges water storage, the one-way annual traffic of the Chuanjiang River was only 10 million tons, and the 10,000-ton ships could not reach Chongqing. The Three Gorges Project ended the history of “the ancient Chuanjiang River does not sail at night”, and the Three Gorges several times of water storage made the navigation conditions of the Chuanjiang River increasingly improved. In 2009, the cargo volume through the Three Gorges Dam was about 70 million tons. Since the opening of the Three Gorges ship lock in 2003, the accumulated dam cargo volume has exceeded 300 million tons, exceeding the total volume of freight shipments in the 22 years before storage.
