The State Council promulgated - "Regulations on the Protection of Electric Power Facilities"

        Published: 2019-09-03         Big mean small
(September 15, 1987, the State Council issued the first revision of the "Decision of the State Council on Amending the Regulations on the Protection of Electric Power Facilities" on January 7, 1998. According to the "Regulations of the State Council on the Abolition and Amendment of Some Administrative Regulations" on January 8, 2011 Decision [1]  second revision) [2] 

Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 These Regulations are formulated　to ensure the smooth progress of power production and construction and to maintain public safety.
　　Article 2　These Regulations apply to power facilities built or under construction in the People's Republic of China (including power generation facilities, substation facilities and power line facilities and related auxiliary facilities, the same below).
　　Article 3　The protection of power facilities shall be governed by the principle of combining power management departments, public security departments, power enterprises and the people.
　　Article 4　Power facilities are protected by national laws and any unit or individual is prohibited from engaging in acts that endanger electrical facilities. Any unit or individual has the obligation to protect power facilities, and has the right to stop and report to the power management department and public security department for acts that endanger the power facilities.
　　Electric power enterprises should strengthen the protection of electric power facilities, and appropriate measures should be taken to stop the acts that endanger the safety of electric power facilities.
　　Article 5 The　power management department of the State Council shall be responsible for the supervision, inspection, guidance and coordination of the protection of electric power facilities.
　　Article　responsibilities of local electric administrative department at or above the county protection of power facilities are:
　　(a) supervise and inspect the implementation of this Ordinance and in accordance with the regulations made thereunder;
　　(b) to carry out publicity and education on the protection of power facilities;
　　( 3. Cooperate with relevant departments and units along the power line to establish a mass protection line organization and improve the responsibility system;
　　(4) Cooperate with the local public security department to take charge of the security and protection of the power facilities in the areas under its jurisdiction.
　　Article 7　Public security departments at all levels shall be responsible for investigating and handling cases of destruction of power facilities or looting or theft of power facilities and equipment. [2] 

Chapter II Protection Scope and Protected Areas of Power Facilities

Article 8　Protection scope of power generation facilities and substation facilities:
　　(1) Facilities in power plants, substations, converter stations, switchyards, etc.;
　　(2) Various special pipelines (ditches) outside power plants and substations , ash storage yards, wells, pumping stations, cooling towers, oil depots, dams, railways, roads, bridges, wharfs, fuel handling facilities, lightning protection devices, fire fighting facilities and related auxiliary facilities;
　　(3) Reservoirs used in hydropower plants, Dams, water intakes, diversion tunnels (including branch openings), diversion channels, surge tanks (towers), open-air high-pressure pipelines, power plants, tailraces, communication facilities between powerhouses and dams, and related ancillary facilities.
　　Article 9　Protection scope of power line facilities:
　　(1) Overhead power lines: towers, foundations, cables, grounding devices, conductors, lightning conductors, fittings, insulators, ladders and foot nails for landing towers, and protective facilities for conductors crossing the navigation channel, Patrol (protection) line station, inspection and maintenance of special roads, ships and bridges, signs and related auxiliary facilities;
　　(2) Power cable lines: overhead, underground, underwater power cables and cable coupling devices, cable ducts, cable tunnels, cables Ditch, cable bridge, cable well, cover plate, access hole, standard stone, waterline sign and related auxiliary facilities;
　　(3) Transformers, capacitors, reactors, circuit breakers, disconnectors, arresters, transformers on power lines , fuses, metering devices, power distribution rooms, box-type substations and related auxiliary facilities;
　　(4) Power dispatching facilities: power dispatching sites, power dispatching communication facilities, power grid dispatching automation facilities, power grid operation control facilities.
　　Article 10　Power line protection zone:
　　(1) Overhead power line protection zone: The wire edge line extends horizontally to the outside and is perpendicular to the area formed by two parallel planes on the ground. The extension distance of the voltage wires of the various levels in the general area is as follows:
　　1-10 kV 5 m
　　35-110 kV 10 m
　　154-330 kV 15 m
　　500 kV 20 m
　　In densely populated areas such as factories, mines and towns, the area of ​​overhead power line protection areas may be slightly smaller than the above regulations. However, the distance extending from the edge of the voltage conductor at each level shall not be less than the sum of the horizontal distance of the conductor edge after the maximum calculated sag and the maximum calculated wind deviation and the safety distance of the wind-offset from the building.
　　(2) Power cable line protection area: The underground cable is the area within two parallel lines formed by 0.75 meters on both sides of the cable line ground standard pile; the submarine cable is generally 2 nautical miles on both sides of the line (100 meters on both sides in the port) ), the river cable is generally not less than the water within the two parallel lines formed by 100 meters on each side of the line (the medium and small rivers are generally not less than 50 meters each). [2] 

Chapter III Protection of Power Facilities

Article 11 The　power management departments at or above the county level shall take the following measures to protect the power facilities:
　　(1) On the boundary of the necessary overhead power line protection zone, a sign shall be established, indicating the width and protection provisions of the protected area. ;
　　(b) in the overhead power lines across important road and waterway sector, should be set up signs and mark the wires from crossing safety distance between the object;
　　after (c) of underground cable laying, up permanent marks, and The location of the underground cable
　　shall be notified in writing to the relevant department; (4) After the laying of the underwater cable, a permanent mark shall be established and the location of the underwater cable shall be notified to the relevant department in writing.
　　Article 12　Any unit or individual that performs blasting operations around electric power facilities must ensure the safety of electric power facilities in accordance with relevant state regulations.
　　Article 13　No unit or individual may engage in the following acts that endanger power generation facilities or substation facilities:
　　(1) Breaking into production plants and substations, disrupting production and work order, moving and damaging markers;
　　(2) jeopardizing water transport, Safe operation of pipelines (ditches) such as oil transportation, heat supply, and ash discharge;
　　(3) affecting the use of special railways, highways, bridges, and wharves;
　　(iv) entering the distance from hydraulic structures in reservoirs used for hydropower generation Fish, fishing, swimming, boating and other acts that may endanger the safety of hydraulic structures in the 300-meter area;
　　(5) Other acts that endanger power generation and substation facilities.
　　Article 14　No unit or individual may engage in the following acts that endanger the power line facilities:
　　(1) shooting at power line facilities;
　　(2) throwing objects at the guide line;
　　(3) 300 meters on both sides of the overhead power line conductors Flying kites in the area;
　　(4) arbitrarily using electrical equipment on the wires;
　　(5) arbitrarily climbing the tower or erecting power lines, communication lines, and broadcasting lines
　　on the tower, and installing broadcast horns; (6) using the towers and cables for lifting and anchoring the ground anchors;
　　(7) smashing livestock and hanging objects on the towers and pull lines , cling crop;
　　(viii) within a predetermined range tower, cable-based earth, piling, drilling or excavation dump acids, bases, salts and other harmful chemicals;
　　(IX) in the tower (the tower and the tower free (Between) or between the tower and the pull line to build the road;
　　(10) dismantle the equipment on the tower or pull line, move or damage the permanent signs or signs;
　　(11) other acts that endanger the power line facilities.
　　Article XV　Any unit or individual overhead power line protection zones, must comply with the following provisions:
　　(a) shall not be stacked grain, forage, waste, slag, flammable, explosive materials and other items affect the safety of supply;
　　( 2) No burning of kiln or burning;
　　(3) Construction of buildings and structures
　　shall not be carried out ; (4) Plants that may endanger the safety of electric power facilities shall not be planted.
　　Article 16　Any unit or individual in the power cable line protection zone must abide by the following provisions:
　　(1) Do not pile up garbage, slag, combustibles or explosives in the underground cable protection zone, and pour acid, alkali and salt. And other harmful chemicals, building buildings, structures or planting trees and bamboo;
　　(2) not anchoring or towing anchors in the submarine cable protection zone;
　　(3) not anchoring, towing anchors or frying fish in the river and river cable protection zone, Dig the sand.
　　Article 17　Any unit or individual must be approved by the local power management department at or above the county level and take safety measures before carrying out the following operations or activities:
　　(1) Carrying out farmland water conservancy basic construction projects in the overhead power line protection zone and Piling, drilling, excavation, etc.
　　(2) Any part of the hoisting machinery enters the overhead power line protection zone for construction;
　　(3) less than the safety distance between the traverse object and the overhead power line protection zone;
　　(4) in the power cable line protection zone operation.
　　Article 18　No unit or individual may engage in the following acts that endanger the construction of electric power facilities:
　　(1) illegally encroaching on land under the law for the construction of electric power facilities;
　　(2) measuring and moving, damaging, and removing the construction of the construction of electric power facilities and Mark;
　　(3) Destroy or block the construction road and cut off the construction water source or power supply.
　　Article 19　No unit or individual may acquire power facilities and equipment without the approval of relevant departments in accordance with relevant state regulations. [2] 

Chapter IV deals with the obstruction of power facilities and other facilities.

Article 20　The construction and protection of electric power facilities shall try to avoid or reduce the losses caused to the state, the collective and the individual.
　　Article 21　Newly built overhead power lines shall not cross areas where warehouses for flammable or explosive materials are stored; generally, they shall not cross houses. When special circumstances need to cross houses, power construction enterprises shall take safety measures and reach an agreement with relevant units.
　　Article 22 When　public works, urban greening and other projects impede power facilities during construction, reconstruction or expansion, or when power facilities interfere with public works, urban greening and other projects during construction, reconstruction or expansion, the relevant units of both parties must In accordance with these Regulations and the relevant provisions of the State, an agreement can be reached on the transfer, taking necessary protective measures and compensation, etc. before construction.
　　Article 23 The　power management department shall notify the competent department of urban and rural construction planning of the approved plans and plans for the construction, reconstruction or expansion of electric power facilities, and delineate the protected areas.
　　The competent department of urban and rural construction planning shall incorporate plans and plans for the construction, reconstruction or expansion of electric power facilities into urban and rural construction plans.
　　Article 24 If a　new or renovated or expanded power facility needs to damage crops, cut down trees, bamboo, or demolish buildings and other facilities, the power construction enterprise shall provide one-time compensation in accordance with relevant state regulations.
　　Electric power enterprises shall be pruned or felled according to law in accordance with the trees and bamboos planted in the protected area of ​​power facilities designated by law or that may naturally endanger the safety of power facilities. [2] 

Chapter V Rewards and Punishments

Article 25　Any unit or individual of the following acts, power management should be commended substance or a one-time award:
　　(a) the destruction of power facilities or looting, theft of power facilities behavior report and expose active;
　　(ii Fighting against the destruction of power facilities or looting and stealing power facilities and equipment, effectively preventing accidents;
　　(3) Fighting natural disasters for the protection of power facilities with outstanding achievements;
　　(4) To maintain the safety of power facilities, Significant achievements.
　　Article 26　Anyone who violates the provisions of these Regulations and conducts blasting or other operations around the power facilities or in the protected area of ​​the power facilities designated according to law, without authorization or taking security measures, endangers the safety of the power facilities, and the power management department Order to stop the operation, restore the original condition and compensate for the loss.
　　Article 27 Anyone who　violates the provisions of these Regulations and jeopardizes power generation facilities, substation facilities and power line facilities shall be ordered to make corrections by the power management department; if they refuse to make corrections, they shall be fined not more than 10,000 yuan.
　　Article 28 In　violation of the provisions of these Regulations, the power management department shall order to stop the kiln burning, burning, anchoring, towing, frying, and dredging operations in the protected area of ​​power facilities designated according to law, which endangers the safety of power facilities. Work, rehabilitate and compensate for losses.
　　Article 29 Anyone who　violates the provisions of these Regulations and endangers the construction of electric power facilities shall be ordered by the electric power management department to correct, restore the original condition and compensate for the loss.
　　Article 30　Any unit or individual that violates the provisions of these Regulations and constitutes a violation of public security management shall be punished by the public security department in accordance with the Law of the People's Republic of China on Public Security Administration Punishment; if a crime is constituted, the judicial organ shall pursue criminal responsibility according to law. [2] 

Chapter VI Supplementary Provisions

Article 31 The　power management department of the State Council may, in conjunction with the relevant departments of the State Council, formulate detailed rules for the implementation of these Regulations.
　　Article 32　These Regulations shall come into force as of the date of promulgation. [2]
