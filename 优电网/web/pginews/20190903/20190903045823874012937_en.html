State Council - "Power System Reform Program"

Notice of the State Council on Printing and Distributing the Reform Plan of the Electric Power System

Guofa [2002] No. 5


The people's governments of all provinces, autonomous regions and municipalities directly under the Central Government, ministries and commissions of the State Council, and their respective agencies:

The State Council has agreed to the "Power System Reform Plan" proposed by the State Planning Commission and relevant departments and units. It is now issued to you, please implement it carefully.

State Council 　　 　　　　　

〇 Er 〇 Ernian Eryue Shi Ri

Power system reform plan


First, the need to accelerate the reform of the power system

(1) Since the reform and opening up, China's power industry has developed rapidly and the power construction has made great achievements. Both power generation capacity and annual power generation have leapt to the second place in the world. Most of the regions have formed a regional high-voltage main grid across provinces. The power shortage has been significantly improved. The power industry has strongly supported the national economy. Rapid development.

(2) After the power supply and demand situation in the power market has changed significantly, the current power system has exposed some shortcomings that do not meet the requirements of the socialist market economic system. The institutional defects of monopoly management are increasingly obvious. The market barriers between provinces hinder the formation of inter-provincial electricity market and the optimal allocation of power resources. The current management methods do not meet the development requirements. In order to promote the development of the power industry and improve the overall competitiveness of the national economy, we must accelerate the process of deepening the reform of the power system.

(3) After decades of development, China's power industry has formed a strong production capacity. Many countries have learned from the experience and lessons of the power system reform. We have implemented a number of power-generating and reform pilot projects in China to further deepen the reform. Accumulated experience. The Party Central Committee and the State Council have attached great importance to the reform of the power system, and all sectors of society have called for accelerated reforms. These have created good conditions for the reform of the power system.

Second, the guiding ideology and goals of the power system reform

(4) The guiding ideology of the reform of the power system is: in accordance with the spirit of the 15th National Congress of the 15th Party and the Fifth Plenary Session of the 15th Central Committee, sum up and learn from the experience and lessons of the reform of the power system at home and abroad, proceed from the national conditions, follow the law of the development of the power industry, fully Give play to the basic role of market allocation of resources, accelerate the improvement of the modern enterprise system, promote the transformation of internal management mechanisms of power companies, and establish an electric power system that is compatible with the socialist market economic system. Reform should be conducive to the development of the power industry, to improve the safety and reliability of power supply, to improve the impact on the environment, and to meet the growing power demand of the whole society. In accordance with the principles of overall design, step-by-step implementation, active and steady, and supporting advancement, we must strengthen leadership, meticulously organize, and complete reform tasks in steps and phases.

(V) The overall objectives of the reform are: breaking monopoly, introducing competition, improving efficiency, reducing costs, improving electricity price mechanism, optimizing resource allocation, promoting power development, promoting nationwide networking, and building separate government, enterprise, and fair competition and openness under government supervision. An orderly and healthy development of the electricity market system.

(VI) The main tasks of the power system reform during the 10th Five-Year Plan period are: implementation of separation of plant and network, reorganization of power generation and power grid enterprises; implementation of bidding online, establishment of electricity market operation rules and government supervision system, preliminary establishment of competitive, open regional electricity market Implement a new electricity price mechanism; formulate environmental protection discount standards for power generation emissions, form a new mechanism to stimulate the development of clean power sources; carry out pilot work for power generation enterprises to directly supply power to large users, change the pattern of power purchases exclusively by power grid enterprises; continue to promote rural power management Institutional reform.

Third, the factory network separates the state-owned power assets separately

(7) Separate the plant network. The assets managed by the State Power Corporation are divided into two categories: power generation and power grid, and assets, finances and personnel are reorganized separately.

Power companies that are managed by local governments and other departments must also be separated from the factory network. In the power supply area where small hydropower is self-sufficient and self-supply, it is necessary to strengthen the construction of the power grid and implement the separation of the plant and network in due course.

(8) Reorganizing the power generation assets managed by the State Power Corporation and establishing a number of independent power generation enterprises in accordance with the requirements for establishing a modern enterprise system. Huaneng Company can be directly reorganized into an independent power generation enterprise, and the remaining power generation assets (including the corresponding share assets of the joint stock company or limited liability company) will be restructured to form three to four national power generation enterprises each with an installed capacity of about 40 million kilowatts. Authorized operations are separately implemented in the national plan.

The reorganization of power generation assets should be based on a comprehensive combination of the asset quality of the power plant and the location conditions. The share of each power generation company in each power market is in principle no more than 20%.

(9) Restructuring of grid assets and establishment of State Grid Corporation. The State Grid Corporation authorized by the State Council as the representative of the power grid assets managed by the former State Power Company. The State Grid Corporation is set up in a state-owned form and is implemented in a separate list in the national plan.

Established power grid companies in North China (including Shandong), Northeast China (including Inner Mongolia), Northwest China, East China (including Fujian), and Central China (including Chongqing and Sichuan). Within the business scope of these regional power grid companies, the former state power company has a large proportion of assets, and its construction work is the responsibility of the State Grid Corporation. Each locality participates in the share of the net assets of the grid, and the regional power grid limited liability company or limited shares are formed. the company. The Tibetan power company is managed by the State Grid Corporation.

Established China Southern Power Grid Corporation. Its business scope is Yunnan, Guizhou, Guangxi, Guangdong and Hainan. Within the scope of the operation of China Southern Power Grid Corporation, the original local power grid assets accounted for a large proportion, and its formation work was undertaken by the controlling party. The board of directors was established according to the proportion of the existing net assets of the parties, and a limited liability company or joint stock limited company was established and responsible for operation and management. A separate line is implemented in the national plan.

According to the modern enterprise system, the regional power grid company has clear property rights, clear powers and responsibilities, separate government and enterprise, scientific management, legal person property rights, and responsibility for asset preservation and appreciation. According to the specific situation of the development of the power market and the reasonable corporate governance structure, the regional power grid company will reorganize the existing provincial power companies in the region into branches or subsidiaries, and be responsible for operating the corresponding local transmission and distribution business. Regional grid companies can have pumped storage power plants or a small number of emergency and peaking power plants. Individual power plants that have not been included in the post-restructuring power generation enterprise may be managed by the regional power grid company.

(10) The main duties of the State Grid Corporation are: to be responsible for power trading and dispatching between regional power grids, to deal with the problem of inter-network coordination in the daily production of regional power grid companies; to participate in cross-regional power transmission and transformation related to investment, construction and operation. And network engineering, recently responsible for the construction and management of the Three Gorges power transmission and transformation network project; commissioned by the relevant state departments to assist in the formulation of national power grid development planning.

(11) The main duties of the regional power grid company are: operating and managing the power grid, ensuring power supply security, planning the development of regional power grids, cultivating regional power markets, managing power dispatching trading centers, and conducting power dispatching according to market rules.

(12) During the “10th Five-Year Plan” period, the grid companies may not restructure the transmission and distribution separately, but gradually implement internal financial independent accounting for the distribution business. At present, a number of power supply enterprises crossing the business district within a county should establish a county power supply limited liability company or a joint stock limited company based on the proportion of the existing distribution network assets. The asset relationship of power supply companies outside the State Power Corporation can maintain the status quo.

(13) Adjusting and reorganizing the auxiliary business units and “three-product” and multi-operating enterprises owned by the current state power company system. Grid companies can have the necessary power research institutions. Businesses other than the main business shall be reported to the relevant state departments for approval in accordance with the prescribed procedures, and shall be accounted for separately from the grid business. Auxiliary business units related to power design, repair, construction, etc., must be decoupled from the grid enterprises, carry out corporate transformation and enter the market. Medical and educational units implement localized management in accordance with national regulations. The “three productions” and the diversification of multi-operating enterprises participating in power generation enterprises can also be managed by local governments.

(14) After the reorganization, all types of power enterprises should pay attention to changing the operating mechanism, optimize the organizational structure, streamline the management level, deepen the reform of the three internal labor, personnel and distribution systems, and establish and improve the internal incentive and restraint mechanism.

(15) After the completion of the separate reorganization of the plant and the network, the power generation and grid enterprises are allowed to go through the capital market listing and financing, and further implement the shareholding system transformation. The realized income of state-owned assets sold by power companies shall be listed in the central and local financial account management according to the principle of the central and local assets in the enterprise and the two lines of revenue and expenditure. In principle, priority shall be given to power construction and improvement of power environmental protection. And monitoring facilities and dealing with the "stranding costs" in the reform of the power system.

(16) The reform of the power system involves the changes and adjustments of the central government and local governments in the financial interests of power companies. The Ministry of Finance studies and formulates solutions.

Fourth, bidding on the Internet to implement a new mechanism of electricity prices

(17) Establish a power dispatching trading center and implement power generation bidding online. Within the scope of operation of the regional power grid company, one or several power dispatching trading centers shall be set up according to the specific conditions of the power grid structure, load distribution characteristics and regional electricity price levels, which shall be managed by the regional power grid company. The market is open to the market between power dispatching and trading centers.

(18) Gradually terminate the power purchase contracts signed by power grid enterprises at the same level and power plants in the past.

(19) All power generation enterprises should try to digest internally the new electricity prices of new and old power plants due to historical reasons or through mergers and acquisitions in the capital market to form new market values ​​of various power plants and achieve equal competition for on-grid electricity prices. It is also possible to use a transitional bidding method within a specified period of time. The specific bidding method can be formulated according to the different situations of each power dispatching trading center.

(20) The progress of bidding online in all regions should be tailored to local conditions, and should be determined according to the grid structure, management level and technical support system. Areas with conditions should establish electricity dispatching trading centers as soon as possible, and bidding online.

(21) Establish a reasonable electricity price formation mechanism. The electricity price is divided into on-grid electricity price, transmission electricity price, distribution electricity price and terminal sales price. The on-grid price is composed of the capacity electricity price set by the state and the electricity price generated by the market bidding; the transmission and distribution price are determined by the government; the sales price is based on the above-mentioned electricity price, and a mechanism for linkage with the on-grid price is established. The government regulates and supervises the price of each link according to the efficiency principle, the incentive mechanism and the requirement of attracting investment, and considering the social affordability.

(22) Pilot work for power generation enterprises to directly supply power to users of higher voltage levels or larger power consumption and distribution networks in areas with conditions. The price of direct power supply is determined by the power generation enterprise and the user, and the national transmission and distribution price is implemented.

V. Establishment of the State Electricity Regulatory Commission

(23) The State Council has a National Electricity Regulatory Commission (ministerial level). The institution is a public institution directly under the State Council and performs its power supervision duties in accordance with the authorization of the state. The Electricity Regulatory Commission is set up in a vertical management system and dispatches representative offices to the regional power grid company's power dispatching trading center.

(24) The main duties of the State Electricity Regulatory Commission are: formulate rules for the operation of the electricity market, supervise the operation of the market, and maintain fair competition; according to market conditions, propose to the government price department to adjust the price of electricity; supervise and inspect the production quality standards of power companies, Issue and manage power business licenses; handle power market disputes; and oversee the implementation of social universal service policies.

6. Promote the reform of the power system step by step

(25) Establish the State Electricity Regulatory Commission as soon as possible. It takes one year to form a national grid company, a regional grid company and various power generation companies. All regional power grid companies should pay close attention to improving the bidding online technical support system and environmental protection real-time monitoring facilities. By the end of the 10th Five-Year Plan, major power generation enterprises in all regions should participate in the unified bidding and implement new electricity price mechanisms in most parts of the country.

(XXVI) After completing the above-mentioned reforms, on the basis of doing a good job of pilot work, the division of transmission and distribution will be gradually implemented, and a competition mechanism will be introduced in the sales of electricity.

(27) With the promotion of “West-to-East Power Transmission” and national networking, we will timely summarize the experience of power system reform and further deepen the institutional reform of regional power grid enterprises.

Seven, improve the supporting measures for the reform of the power system

(28) Formulate and revise laws, regulations and other relevant administrative regulations on electricity and electricity prices in a timely manner. As soon as possible, formulate electricity market operation rules, electricity market supervision methods, and power generation emission reduction standards, and implement them at the same time as bidding.

(29) Transforming government functions. Give full play to the basic role of market allocation of resources, improve the government's macro-control, reform the power project approval methods, strengthen the power market supervision, and do a good job in the early stage of power construction projects under the guidance of the national medium- and long-term planning.

(30) Further enrich and improve the self-discipline, coordination, supervision and service functions of the China Electricity Council, and give full play to its role as a bridge and link between the government, society and power companies. Authorized by the government to perform duties such as statistics and analysis of power industry information and materials.

8. Strengthening the organizational leadership of the power system reform

(31) Under the leadership of the State Council, the establishment of the State Planning Commission, the State Economic and Trade Commission, the State Power Corporation, the Central Organization Department, the Central Enterprise Work Committee, the China Office, the Ministry of Finance, the Legal Affairs Office, the Office of Economic Reform, and Guangdong The power system reform working group composed of the provincial and proposed National Electricity Regulatory Commission and other relevant departments and units is specifically responsible for the implementation of the power system reform. According to the principle of active and prudent organization, we will distinguish the different situations of various regions and power companies, and focus on the implementation steps and specific measures for the transition period, and promote the reform step by step under the overall design. Relevant government departments should strengthen guidance on reform, and local governments at all levels should actively cooperate.

(32) In the process of reform, power companies should further strengthen leadership, do a good job in political thinking and propaganda, strengthen organizational discipline, and maintain the ideology of cadres and workers. For the disposal of state-owned assets, state regulations must be strictly enforced to prevent loss. All power companies must clarify their responsibilities, stick to their posts, obey the dispatch, and ensure the safe operation of the power grid.
