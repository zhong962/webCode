Special call: Power charging giant of the national grid

        Published: 2019-08-23         Big mean small

The battle of the first half of the group is nearing the end, charging the piles of rivers and lakes or entering the oligarchy era of large-scale housing enterprises, car companies and energy companies.

Text / Shi Yufei

Edit / Su Ling

Yu Dexiang sat on the throne of the king of the charging pile. He is in charge of the special call New Energy Co., Ltd. (hereinafter referred to as "special call") to force the national grid, star charging and other players, to seize nearly half of the charging pile industry.

But recent developments in several industries may have made him feel like a needle.

Not long ago, the National Grid and the Evergrande Group jointly established the State Grid Evergrande Smart Energy Co., Ltd. (hereinafter referred to as “State Grid Evergrande”. On the day of the establishment of the new company, four real estate giants such as Evergrande, Country Garden, Vanke and Sunac Establish alliances and seek common cause of charging piles.

A few months ago, the Chinese traveler "One Brother" who had just "breakup" with the special call, joined forces with the multinational oil giant BP, and stirred up the pile of rivers and lakes.

However, compared with the strong newcomers, Yu Dexiang’s painstaking efforts for five years have begun to take effect. Four months ago, the "Pig King" announced to the outside world that the special call was finally profitable for the first time in 2018.

However, more independent charging pile operators have left the scene in the war escalation. The battle of the first half of the group is nearing the end, charging the piles of rivers and lakes or entering the oligarchy era of large-scale housing enterprises, car companies and energy companies.

Yu Dexiang spent five years of hard work and finally stood out in the martyrdom. But now the pattern has changed. How will this independent operator break through in the giants' attack and firmly hold the tickets for the second half?

"Pile King"

Yu Dexiang’s sense of crisis was first seen five months ago.

On March 30th, Special Call issued a notice on its APP platform, announcing that since 0:00 on April 1st, it will no longer charge the charging service for Drips.

The other two head operators, Star Charging and Wanma Aid, also announced the contract with Didi.

According to the three companies, according to the original cooperation agreement, Xiaotang Charging only provides platform services, and should not be involved in the construction of offline charging stations and compete with operators. However, Didi did not follow the agreement, robbing users online and online.

Drip is obviously not willing to be bound by a paper association. As early as last June, Xiaotang Charging set up the first batch of cooperative stations in the Beijing Railway Cultural and Art Corps and Nanjing Yingtian Auchan, officially launched the offline battle.

The siege under the drip online caused a strong dissatisfaction with operators such as special calls. The game between the two sides ended in the termination of the agreement.

But Didi quickly introduced another, more powerful ally, BP.

Earlier this month, Didi and BP announced the establishment of a joint venture to jointly develop and operate a new energy vehicle charging station in China. At present, the first pile station built by BP in Guangzhou has been connected to the Xiaotian charging platform of Didi, and has been trial-operated.

Just before the launch of a new round of attacks by Didi, the national grid also increased its layout. According to statistics from the China Electric Vehicle Charging Infrastructure Promotion Alliance, as of June 2019, the number of charging grids for the second-largest national grid operation was 87,800 units, far behind the 133,100 units of special calls.

But the world's largest utility is far-sighted. This year, the company has listed the ubiquitous power Internet of Things as its "most urgent and important task." As an important part of the ubiquitous power Internet of Things, the State Grid is committed to the charging pile business.

In Shenzhen, 2000 kilometers away from Beijing, Xu Jiayin is anxiously waiting for the arrival of Xindian, the general manager of the State Grid. The richest man in China, who has invested hundreds of billions of gambling new energy vehicles, is counting on speeding up the new track by marrying the national grid.

State Grid and Evergrande Group each hold 50% of the national network Evergrande was born.

On the day of the unveiling, Country Garden President Mo Bin, Vanke Group President Zhu Jiusheng, and Sunac China President Wang Mengde rarely gathered at Evergrande Headquarters to sign a contract to jointly promote the construction of community charging piles.

This powerful alliance has emerged, covering 5,468 communities across the country, 8.72 million households, and about 31 million homeowners. The living space of independent operators such as special calls will be further squeezed.

However, Yu Dexiang is very pleased that the special call has taken the lead in achieving profit before the giants poured in.

In April of this year, Yu Dexiang wrote a letter to the shareholders of the special caller parent company, Ruide (300001.SZ). The letter stated that in the past five years, the cumulative investment of special calls has exceeded 5 billion yuan, and the accumulated losses in the first four years have reached 600 million yuan. The sector finally crossed the break-even line in 2018 and began to make a profit.

“The current income of this industry is still relatively simple, mainly relying on charging service fees. In theory, if the site selection is good, the utilization rate increase can be profitable.” Special call insiders introduced to “Wangma Energy”, The development and manufacture of power equipment started, which made the special call superior in technology and manufacturing.

The official caller responded to "Wangma Energy", saying that the company has advantages such as technology and cloud platform. Since its inception, the strategic goal of the special call is to build a charging network, not just a charging pile.

In the past five years, under the guidance of this blueprint, Yu Dexiang led the special call to step into the "iron throne" of the charging pile industry.

Five years of king

Yu Dexiang is known for his sense of smell in the industry.

As early as 2004, he resigned from the system and resigned from the sea to create the company, and entered the field of emerging substation and power distribution equipment. Key projects such as the Qinghai-Tibet Railway and the Beijing-Tianjin High-speed Railway are not lacking.

Terad's market share in the railway power equipment market was close to 60%. After the launch of the GEM five years later, Terad was hailed by the capital market as the “first stock of the GEM”.
