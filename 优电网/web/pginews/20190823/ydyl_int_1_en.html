State Grid and Nepal Power Authority signed a China-Nigeria Power Network Feasibility Study Cooperation Agreement

        Published：2018-06-22        大中小

On June 20, witnessed by Nepalese Prime Minister Ollie, General Manager of China State Grid Corporation, Wei Wei, and General Manager of Nepal Power Bureau, Ji Xin, signed the “China-Nigeria Power Network Feasibility Study Cooperation Agreement” in Beijing, marking the China-Nigeria Power Networking Project. Enter the engineering feasibility study stage. This is another major progress made by the State Grid Corporation in implementing the concept of a community of human destiny, actively promoting the construction of the “Belt and Road” and promoting the interconnection and interconnection of neighboring countries.

After the signing ceremony, Wei Wei held talks with Nepal’s Minister of Energy, Basha Man Phuen. Wei Wei said that the State Grid Corporation actively implemented the “One Belt, One Road” initiative to promote interconnection and interconnection with neighboring national power grids. State Grid Corporation has rich experience in the field of investment, construction and operation of power grids and world-leading technology. In recent years, it has actively carried out preliminary work on China-Nigeria Power Network, and has basically completed project pre-feasibility study. In the next step, the two sides should speed up project feasibility studies and advance new breakthroughs in the China-Nigeria power interconnection project. State Grid Corporation is willing to take the lead in carrying out the feasibility study of China-Nepal Power Network. It is hoped that the Nigerian side will fully support the work of the State Grid Corporation in collecting and designing, and smoothly advance the project to achieve substantial progress.

Puen spoke highly of the development capabilities and technical level of the State Grid Corporation. He said that Prime Minister Ollie and the Nepalese government attach great importance to China-Nepalese power and energy cooperation, and the two countries have cooperated smoothly in the field of power transmission and distribution. The China-Nigeria Power Networking Project is an important project for energy cooperation between the two countries. The signing of the China-Netherlands Power Network Feasibility Study Cooperation Agreement is of great significance to the implementation of the “Belt and Road Initiative” initiative by the two governments to promote the infrastructure of neighboring countries. The Ministry of Energy of Nepal will fully support and cooperate with the State Grid Corporation to carry out the feasibility study of China-Nepal Power Network, and hopes to achieve results as soon as possible.

During Ori's visit to China in March 2016, the Chinese and Nigerian governments issued a joint statement: agreed to establish an energy cooperation dialogue mechanism to better carry out long-term cooperation planning, including cross-border power grids; Nepal hopes that China will build cross-border power transmission. The electric project and asked the Chinese to make the project a priority. The company started the project pre-feasibility study in 2016. It plans to build a DC back-to-back converter station in the border area between China and Nepal, and build a converter station to the AC transmission line in Kathmandu, Nepal.

Zhao Qingbo, assistant general manager of the company, Zhu Guangchao, deputy chief engineer and director of the International Cooperation Department, and the head of the State Grid Development Department, the head of the CLP Equipment Company attended the meeting.
