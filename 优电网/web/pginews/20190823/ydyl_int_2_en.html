State Grid implements the “One Belt, One Road” strategy, China-Pakistan Power Interconnection Speed ​​Increase

        Published:2014/11/20        大中小

Polaris Smart Grid Online News: The reporter learned from the State Grid Xinjiang Electric Power Company that in early November, the State Grid Corporation issued 20 support opinions on the development of Xinjiang Power, with the focus on implementing the “One Belt, One Road” strategic deployment.

"The State Grid will select Xinjiang as an important node to implement the 'One Belt, One Road' strategy, take the lead in constructing the Silk Road Economic Belt Transmission Corridor, and build a transmission channel from Xinjiang to the five Central Asian countries. Increase cooperation with neighboring countries' power projects, The China-Pakistan Economic Corridor Power Interconnection Project is about to start." State Grid insiders revealed to reporters yesterday.

It is understood that the State Grid 20 supports Xinjiang's power measures, mainly focusing on continuously increasing investment in power grids and accelerating the implementation of UHV power grids and 750 kV grid construction.

The State Grid Xinjiang Electric Power Company reported that the Xinjiang 750 kV backbone network project will be officially launched in the near future. After the completion of the project at the end of 2015, the total investment will be 14.4 billion yuan. After the completion of the project, the capacity of “East Power Transmission” will be further enhanced.

"The power of Xinjiang Electric Power will be the first to benefit from Pakistan, and the China-Brazil power transmission project is about to start."

In the China-Pakistan Economic Corridor Cooperation Project that has been finalized, in addition to roads and railways, another big part is the interconnection of oil and gas pipelines and power grids.

“The China-Pakistan Economic Corridor that has just been signed contains a number of power cooperation projects.” Pakistan’s planner and development department spokesman Zamir recently revealed that China’s State Grid will build at least four power stations in Pakistan.

Zamir said, "Investment in energy projects in Pakistan comes from China's investment funds, not loans and aid. The $40 billion Silk Road Fund established by China will become the fund pool for future infrastructure."

The deployment of China-Brazil energy interconnection has already started before the APEC meeting. During the meeting, the China-Pakistan Economic Corridor was defined as the “flagship project” for interconnection. In January this year, the third meeting of the China-Pakistan Energy Working Group and the first meeting of the Energy Working Group of the China-Pakistan Economic Corridor was held in Beijing to study and formulate the energy cooperation plan for the China-Pakistan Economic Corridor, promote the construction of corridor energy infrastructure, and interconnect the power grid. Key project list and safeguard mechanism.
