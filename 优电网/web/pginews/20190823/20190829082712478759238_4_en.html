Analysis of China's Power Development and Reform Situation

        Published: 2019-08-23         Big mean small

According to the National Energy Administration, in 2018, the utilization rate of renewable energy including hydropower, wind power, photovoltaic power generation, biomass power generation, etc. in China has increased significantly, and the situation of abandoned water, abandoned wind and abandoned light has been significantly alleviated. In 2018, China's abandoned wind power was 27.7 billion kWh, a year-on-year decrease of 14.2 billion kWh. The wind curtailment rate dropped by 5 percentage points year-on-year. Most of the areas with severe wind curtailment have improved, and the winds are mainly concentrated in Xinjiang, Gansu and Inner Mongolia. In 2018, the national abandoned photoelectric output decreased by 1.8 billion kWh, and the light rejection rate dropped by 2.8 percentage points year-on-year. The abandoned photoelectric output and the light rejection rate were “double down”, and the abandoned light was mainly concentrated in Xinjiang and Gansu.

Fourth, the scale of the national power grid continued to expand, ranking the world's largest power grid

According to CEC data, in 2018, the newly added 220 kV and above substation equipment capacity was 221 million kVA, and the new growth rate decreased compared with the previous year. By the end of 2018, the national capacity of 220 kV and above substation equipment reached 4.023 billion kVA, with a growth rate of 6.15%.

In 2018, the length of the newly added transmission line of 220 kV and above was 41,000 kilometers, and the new amount was equivalent to that of the previous year. By the end of 2018, the length of the national 220 kV and above transmission line circuit reached 733,400 kilometers, an increase of 7.0%.

From the data of the past ten years, the scale of power grid expansion is obvious. The newly added capacity of 220 kV and above has exceeded 200 million kVA, of which 268 million kVA was added in 2009; the length of new 220 kV and above transmission lines remained above 33,000 km. In 2010, the number of new additions was 44,700 kilometers. In 2011~2016, the annual increase was less than 40,000 kilometers. In 2017 and 2018, the new quantity will return to more than 40,000 kilometers.

Since the grid scale exceeded the United States in 2009, the position of China's largest grid has been further consolidated. Compared with the 2009 value, the substation capacity has increased by 1.28 times and the line length has increased by 0.82 times, ranking the first largest power grid.

5. The nation's power investment continued to shrink, and the gap between power grid and power investment increased.

(1) Total power investment exceeds 800 billion yuan, shrinking for two consecutive years

According to CEC data, in 2018, the national power infrastructure investment completed 272.1 billion yuan, and the power grid infrastructure investment was 537.3 billion yuan. The two total investments reached 809.4 billion yuan, which was more than 800 billion yuan for the fourth consecutive year, but it has been reduced for two consecutive years, a decrease of 14.5 billion yuan compared with 2017, a decrease of 74.5 billion yuan compared with 2016.

From the data of the past ten years, the national power investment has fluctuated, and it has been reduced year by year after reaching the peak in 2016. Since 2009, power investment has remained at more than 700 billion yuan for six consecutive years. In 2015, the nation's power investment exceeded 800 billion yuan for the first time. In 2016, it exceeded 880 billion yuan, ranking the highest in history. 2017, 2018, but still above 800 billion yuan.
