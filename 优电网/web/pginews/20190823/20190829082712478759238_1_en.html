Analysis of China's Power Development and Reform Situation

        Published: 2019-08-23         Big mean small

At the end of February this year, the National Bureau of Statistics released the "Statistical Communiqué on National Economic and Social Development in 2018". In 2018, China's economic operation was generally stable, stable, and steadily improved, quality and efficiency improved steadily, people's lives continued to improve, and the economy continued to develop healthily. The overall situation of the society is stable. According to preliminary calculations, the GDP for the whole year of 2018 was 900.309 billion yuan, an increase of 6.6% over the previous year. Converted at the annual average exchange rate, accounting for about 16% of the global economy, ranking the second largest economy in the world. According to estimates by the International Monetary Fund, the Chinese economy contributed 30% of the increase in world economic growth in 2018.

(Source: Energy Research Club ID: nyqbyj Author: Cui Xiaoli Wangxue Chen)

The energy and power industry is a basic industry of the national economy, and its level of development is directly related to the national economy and people's livelihood. In 2018, China's power industry developed rapidly and all work was steadily advanced. According to the communique, the total energy consumption in 2008 was 4.64 billion tons of standard coal, an increase of 3.3% over the previous year, of which power consumption increased by 8.5%. In 2018, China's power supply and demand is generally balanced, the growth rate of social power consumption is accelerating, the power consumption structure is further optimized, and the power industry is developing toward a green low-carbon quality. With the deepening of reforms, the market has a stronger background, and the industry is facing new situations and new opportunities.

The 2019 anniversary of the founding of New China is a crucial year for building a well-off society in an all-round way. It is vital to do all the work well. In the wave of reforms in the new era, how do power industry companies grasp market trends, optimize structure, integrate advantages, and explore new growth points in new models, which require diligence, dedication, and exploration.

1. The electricity consumption of the whole society is growing rapidly, and the power consumption structure is continuously optimized.

(1) The electricity consumption of the whole society increased by 8.5%, and the growth rate reached a new high of nearly seven years.

In 2018, the electricity consumption of the whole society in China has achieved rapid growth. According to the China Electricity Council Express, in 2018, the national total electricity consumption was 6.84 trillion kWh, an increase of 8.5% year-on-year and 1.9 percentage points year-on-year, the highest growth rate since 2012. The per capita electricity consumption in the country is 4,956 kWh, and the per capita electricity consumption is 701 kWh.

In terms of growth rate, the growth rate is more than 3 percentage points expected at the beginning of the year. According to the forecast of CEC at the beginning of the year, power consumption in 2018 will continue to maintain a steady and rapid growth in 2017. It is estimated that the whole society's electricity consumption will increase by about 5.5%, and the actual growth rate is 8.5%, a record high of nearly seven years. In general, the continuous advancement of electric energy substitution policies, high-tech, equipment manufacturing, new demand for electricity in the Internet and related service industries, and rapid growth in urban and rural residents' electricity consumption have jointly promoted the growth of electricity consumption in the whole society in 2018. Created a new high in nearly seven years.

(2) The growth rate of electricity consumption in the tertiary industry tops the list, and the power consumption structure continues to be optimized.

In 2018, the electricity consumption of various industries showed a relatively strong growth momentum. In terms of industries, the electricity consumption of the primary industry was 72.8 billion kWh, up 9.8% year-on-year; the electricity consumption of the second industry was 472.35 billion kWh, up 7.2% year-on-year, of which industrial electricity consumption was 4,465.6 billion kWh, up 7.1% year-on-year. The electricity consumption of the tertiary industry was 10801 billion kWh, a year-on-year increase of 12.7%, continuing the double-digit growth trend; the electricity consumption of urban and rural residents was 968.5 billion kWh, a year-on-year increase of 10.4%.

In the past ten years, the growth rate of electricity consumption in the tertiary industry has continued to maintain a relatively high growth rate of more than 6%, and fluctuations in the growth rate of electricity consumption in other industries are more obvious. The primary industry electricity consumption experienced negative growth in 2012 and 2014. The secondary industry has both ultra-high-speed growth of 15.9% in 2010 and negative growth in 2015, which is in line with the overall growth rate of electricity consumption in the whole society.

Since the data of 2017 and 2018 have been reclassified according to the new standards, after the adjustment of the “agriculture, forestry, animal husbandry and fishery services” of the primary industry to the tertiary industry, the growth rate of electricity consumption in the primary and tertiary industries cannot be compared with that before 2016. ratio. In 2018, compared with 2017, the growth rate of the tertiary industry and residential electricity consumption exceeded double digits, and the growth rate of primary electricity consumption reached 9.8%.

From the perspective of electricity consumption structure, the total electricity consumption of the tertiary industry in 2017 and 2018 exceeded the total electricity consumption of residents. After dividing the three industries with the new standard, it can more directly reflect the trend of new and old kinetic energy conversion and electricity structure changes. According to the previous classification criteria, since 2009, the growth rate of electricity consumption in the tertiary industry has continued to be higher than the growth rate of residential electricity consumption.

In recent years, China's electricity consumption structure shows a trend of lowering the proportion of primary and secondary industries, and increasing the proportion of tertiary industry and urban and rural residents. From the comparison data of 2017 and 2018, the proportion of the secondary industry is more obvious, and the proportion of electricity consumption of the tertiary industry and urban and rural residents has increased to varying degrees.
