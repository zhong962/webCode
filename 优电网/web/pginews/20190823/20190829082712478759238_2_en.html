Analysis of China's Power Development and Reform Situation

        Published: 2019-08-23         Big mean small

Second, the national power generation capacity growth rate slows down, the power structure is green and upgraded

(1) The national power installation growth rate slowed down, and the newly added capacity exceeded 100 million kilowatts for six consecutive years.

By the end of 2018, the national installed capacity of full-scale power generation was 1.90 billion kilowatts, a year-on-year increase of 6.5%, a decrease of 1.1 percentage points from the previous year. The newly added power generation capacity is 124.39 million kilowatts, which is shrinking compared to 2017, but the power generation capacity has continued to increase the capacity of more than 100 million kilowatts in the past six years.

Figure 6 National power installation and growth rate from 2009 to 2018 (unit: 10,000 kW, %)

Judging from the total installed capacity, the national installed capacity of electric power was 1.90 billion kilowatts at the end of 2018, making it the world's largest installed power. In terms of growth, it increased by 6.5% in 2018, the lowest in the past decade. During the “Eleventh Five-Year Plan” period, China’s power installed capacity continued to grow at a high level of more than double digits. The basic growth rate after the “Twelfth Five-Year Plan” did not exceed 10%, with the exception of 10.62% in 2015. In 2018, the growth rate of power installed capacity was low, which was equivalent to 6.6% of GDP growth rate, which was lower than the growth rate of electricity consumption in the whole society by 2 percentage points.

From the scale of new power generation installed capacity, there is a trend of fluctuation. In 2006, China's newly added power installed capacity exceeded 100 million kilowatts for the first time, but it has been affected by the "big pressure" and coal power dilemma. In 2013, driven by factors such as relatively weak coal prices and extraordinary expansion of new energy power generation, China's power installed capacity has once again entered an era of over 100 million annual growth and continues to be maintained. The national power generation installed capacity has increased from 790 million kilowatts in 2008 to 1.90 billion kilowatts in 2018, with an average annual growth of 111 million kilowatts in a decade, which is growing rapidly.

(II) The green transformation of power generation and installation continued to advance, and the installed structure was further optimized.

In the energy transformation, clean low carbon is an important development direction. With the rise of low-carbon, clean energy sources such as wind power and solar power generation in China, the power supply structure is constantly changing.

In terms of types, hydropower installed capacity is 350 million kilowatts in 2018, thermal power is 1.14 billion kilowatts, nuclear power is 44.66 million kilowatts, grid-connected wind power is 180 million kilowatts, and grid-connected solar power is 170 million kilowatts. Compared with 2017, the proportion of thermal power is further reduced, and the proportion of wind power, photovoltaics and nuclear power has increased significantly. Compared with 2008, in the past ten years, the proportion of thermal power installed capacity has dropped by nearly 16 percentage points, and the proportion of wind power and optoelectronics has increased significantly.

In thermal power installed capacity, coal power generation was 1.01 billion kilowatts in 2018, accounting for 53.0% of the total installed capacity, down 2.2 percentage points from the previous year; gas and electricity was 83.3 million kilowatts, an increase of 10.0%.

From the ten-year historical data, the power supply structure optimization and adjustment trend is obvious, the proportion of traditional fossil energy power generation installed capacity has dropped significantly, and the proportion of new energy installed capacity has increased significantly. Combined with data calculation, the proportion of hydropower and nuclear power installed capacity has changed little in recent years, and thermal power, wind power and photoelectricity have changed significantly. In 2018, the proportion of thermal power installed capacity decreased by 15.9 percentage points compared with 2008. The proportion of wind power, photovoltaic, hydropower and nuclear power generation increased. 15.84 percentage points, hydropower, wind power, solar power and other single installed capacity is the highest in the world.

(3) The new installed capacity of solar power and thermal power has contracted slightly but still has a heavy weight, and nuclear power is growing rapidly.

The newly installed power generation capacity of the country was 120 million kilowatts, a decrease of 6.05 million kilowatts from the previous year, but the newly added non-fossil energy power generation installed capacity accounted for 73.0% of the total installed capacity. At the same time, non-fossil energy generation in 2018 was 2.16 trillion kWh, an increase of 11.1% year-on-year, accounting for 30.9% of total power generation. The national grid-connected solar power, wind power and nuclear power generation capacity were 177.5 billion, 366 billion, and 294.4 billion kWh, respectively, up 50.8%, 20.2%, and 18.6% respectively. Non-fossil energy power supply capacity is enhanced.

In 2018, China's new installed capacity was led by solar power installed capacity, accounting for 36.0% of all new installed capacity; thermal power installed capacity accounted for 33.1%, mainly in Guangdong, Henan, Shaanxi, Shanxi, Anhui and Jiangsu provinces; wind power The newly installed capacity accounted for 16.9%, mainly concentrated in Hebei, Henan, Inner Mongolia, Jiangsu and other provinces; hydropower new installed capacity accounted for 6.9%, mainly concentrated in Yunnan, Sichuan and other provinces.

The China Electricity Council predicts that the installed capacity of new infrastructure in China's infrastructure is expected to reach 110 million kilowatts in 2019. Among them, the newly added non-fossil energy power generation installed capacity is about 62 million kilowatts, and the proportion of non-fossil energy installed capacity is further increased.
