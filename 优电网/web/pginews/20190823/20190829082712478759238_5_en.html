Analysis of China's Power Development and Reform Situation

        Published: 2019-08-23         Big mean small

Judging from the growth rate of national power investment, it has experienced violent fluctuations in the past decade and has experienced negative growth in four years. The peak growth rate reached 22.22% in 2009, and the low was in 2016, with a negative growth of 6.8%. The gap between the two is nearly 30%. In 2018, power investment was a negative growth of 1.8%, which was a rebound from the growth rate of the previous year.

(II) The power investment structure continues to adjust, and the gap in investment in the network source is widened

In 2018, the grid investment reached 53.73 billion yuan, and the year-on-year growth rate turned positive to 0.6%. The power investment was 272.1 billion yuan, which fell again on the basis of the decline in 2017. It fell to below 300 billion yuan for two consecutive years, a sharp drop of 6.2% year-on-year, and the slowdown slowed down. Power grid investment accounted for 66.4%, power investment accounted for 33.6%, this ratio once again broke through last year's proportion structure, the proportion of network source investment further widened the gap.

From the investment trend of the past decade, the power grid and power investment have fluctuated, and the gap between the two has gradually increased in the past five years. Power investment showed volatility. After 2015, it fell for three consecutive years. Compared with 2015, the power investment in 2018 decreased by over 120 billion yuan.

In terms of grid investment, in recent years, grid investment has generally shown an expanding trend. In 2009, it broke through the 300 billion yuan mark for the first time and remained at more than 340 billion yuan for five consecutive years. In 2014, it increased to more than 400 billion yuan. In 2016, it will go up to a level of more than 500 billion yuan. In 2017 and 2018, the grid investment is still above 500 billion yuan, maintaining a high level of investment.

(3) Hydropower investment rose year-on-year, thermal power investment continued to decline

From the perspective of investment in different power sources, the growth rate varies greatly. Among them, hydropower investment was 67.4 billion yuan, with a growth rate of 8.4%; thermal power investment was 77.7 billion yuan, down 9.4% year-on-year; nuclear power investment was 43.7 billion yuan, down 3.8% year-on-year; wind power investment was 64.2 billion yuan, down 5.7% year-on-year.

Compared with the growth rate in 2017, the decline in thermal power investment has narrowed, and it is still at a low investment point in the past decade. In the same situation, nuclear power is the same as the investment low. Hydropower is different, and the amount of investment has risen, but it is still only about half of the high peak of 123.9 billion yuan in 2012, a decrease of 56.5 billion yuan. Compared with the high peak of investment, thermal power has decreased by 76.7 billion yuan from the peak investment of 154.4 billion yuan in 2009. The nuclear power has decreased by 34.7 billion yuan from the investment of 78.4 billion yuan in 2012. The wind power investment in 2015 was reduced by RMB 55.8 billion. .

From “strengthening the overcapacity of coal-fired power generation” to “strictly controlling the scale of new production capacity”, the impact of coal-fired power generation capacity is also evident in thermal power investment. In 2018, the national thermal power investment decreased by 9.4% year-on-year. The country's new coal-fired power generation was 29.03 million kilowatts, and the year-on-year production was 6.01 million kilowatts, which is the lowest level since 2004. The construction speed and scale have been effectively controlled.

In 2018, hydropower investment rose, with a growth rate of 8.4%. The construction of large-scale hydropower stations such as Wudongde and Baihetan in the middle and upper reaches of the Yangtze River has been smoothly promoted, which has driven the investment scale of hydropower power construction.

In terms of nuclear power, investment in 2018 continues to decline in investment. If the approval of nuclear power projects is accelerated in 2019, the construction of the project will start smoothly, and the investment power of the nuclear power industry will be strengthened.

Sixth, the main energy consumption indicators have declined, and the world's largest clean coal power supply system has been built.

The standard coal consumption of power supply continues to decline. According to the data released by the National Energy Administration, the national coal consumption standard coal consumption of 308 g/kWh in 2018 is down by 1 g/kWh compared with the same period of last year. Compared with the 345 g/kWh in 2008, the national coal consumption standard coal consumption has dropped by 37 grams. / kWh, showing a significant downward trend. As the depth of standard coal consumption declines, the annual average reduction begins to shrink, but from the perspective of the decline, the overall trend is declining. The coal consumption of coal-fired units in China has been lower than the planned target of “the average coal consumption of coal-fired generating units under normal operation is less than 310 grams of standard coal/kWh” in the “13th Five-Year Plan for Power Development”.

The national line loss rate dropped slightly, hitting a new low in recent years. In 2018, the national line loss rate was 6.21%, a year-on-year decrease of 0.27 percentage points. In the past ten years, the line loss rate has been declining, but the decline is not obvious, and the cumulative reduction in the decade is 0.58 percentage points. However, in the case of electricity consumption of more than 6 trillion kWh in the whole society, this transcript is equivalent to saving 39.7 billion kWh per year, which is 7 billion kWh higher than that of Hainan Province in 2018.
