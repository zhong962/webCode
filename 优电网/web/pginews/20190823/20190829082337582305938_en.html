Helping the “Belt and Road” The first coal-fired power integration project of the “China-Pakistan Economic Corridor” was put into commercial operation

        Published: 2019-08-11         Big mean small

Recently, the first coal-electricity integration project of the “China-Pakistan Economic Corridor” – the 1st and 2nd units of the 2×330,000 kilowatt CFB coal-fired power station in the first phase of Pakistan’s Taal Power Station, passed the 168-hour trial operation and officially put into commercial operation. ). The generator, steam turbine and control equipment of the project are provided by Dongfang Electric.

The 2×330,000 KW CFB coal-fired power station project of Pakistan’s Taal Power Station is the first batch of priority projects for the “One Belt and One Road” flagship project, “China-Pakistan Economic Corridor”, and the first coal-electricity integration project in Pakistan. The first project in the energy plan to formally implement and realize power generation, the first power station project in Pakistan to burn local coal.

The successful commissioning of the first phase of the Tal Power Plant in Pakistan is the result of the cooperation between China and Pakistan and is a model for the success of government and social capital cooperation (PPP). After the two units are put into production, they can achieve an average annual power generation of about 4.5 billion kWh, which can meet the demand of nearly 2 million households in Pakistan, greatly alleviate the current shortage of electricity in Pakistan, and change the dilemma of Pakistan's dependence on imported oil and natural gas for thermal power generation. It has far-reaching impact on Pakistan's adjustment of power and energy structure, reduction of power generation costs, easing of power supply and demand conflicts, optimization of investment environment, promotion of infrastructure construction and economic development, and improvement of people's livelihood.
