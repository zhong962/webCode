Strong smart grid - promote energy conservation and emission reduction, develop low carbon economy

        Published: 2019-09-05         Big mean small

Strong smart grid construction is of great significance for promoting energy conservation and emission reduction and developing a low-carbon economy: 1 Supporting large-scale access to clean energy units, accelerating the development of clean energy, and promoting the optimal adjustment of China's energy structure; 2 guiding users to rationally arrange electricity consumption periods and reduce Peak load, stabilize the output of thermal power units, reduce coal consumption for power generation; 3 Promote the promotion and application of advanced technologies such as UHV, flexible transmission, and economic dispatch, reduce the transmission loss rate, and improve the economic efficiency of power grid; 4. Realize effective interaction between power grid and users. Intelligent electricity technology to improve electricity efficiency; 5 promote the large-scale application of electric vehicles, promote the development of low-carbon economy, and achieve emission reduction benefits.

The development of the smart grid concept has three milestones:

The first is the "smart grid" solution proposed by IBM in the United States in 2006. IBM's smart grid is mainly to solve the safe operation of the power grid and improve reliability. It can be seen from the white paper "Building Smart Grid Innovation Operation Management - New Ideas for China's Power Development" released in China that the program provides a large framework. Optimized management of all aspects of power production, transportation, and retailing provides a blueprint for related companies to improve operational efficiency and reliability, and reduce costs. It is a marketing strategy of IBM.

The second is the energy plan proposed by Obama after taking office. In addition to the announced plans, the United States will focus on upgrading the grid system that consumes $120 billion annually in circuit loss and fault repair, establishing the US across four time zones. Unified power grid; develop smart grid industry, maximize the value and efficiency of the US national grid, and gradually realize unified network management of solar, wind and geothermal energy in the United States; comprehensively promote distributed energy management and create the world's highest energy efficiency .

It can be seen that the US government's smart grid has three purposes. One is because the US grid equipment is relatively backward, and it is urgent to carry out renovation and transformation to improve the reliability of grid operation. Second, it is to pull the US out of the financial crisis through smart grid construction; Improve energy efficiency.

The third is the “interactive grid” proposed by Chinese energy expert Wu Jiandong. The interactive grid, English is the Interactive Smart Grid, which covers the meaning of the smart grid. The interactive grid is defined as: on the basis of open and interconnected information models, power systems such as power generation, transmission, power supply, electricity consumption, customer sales, grid grading scheduling, and integrated services are realized by loading system digital equipment and upgrading the grid network management system. The intelligent, informational and hierarchical interactive management of the whole process is a comprehensive efficiency change that combines the industrial revolution, the technological revolution and the management revolution. It will re-create the information loop of the power grid, build a new user feedback method, promote the overall transformation of the power grid into energy-saving infrastructure, improve energy efficiency, reduce customer costs, reduce greenhouse gas emissions, and maximize the value of the grid.
