How smart grids change our lives

        Published: 2019-09-05         Tai Zhong small

Original title: How does the smart grid change our lives ?

At present, smart grid construction is in full swing in various places. According to the “State Grid Intelligent Planning General Report (Revised Draft)” issued by State Grid Corporation in March 2010 , the total investment of the State Grid from 2009 to 2020 is 3.45 trillion yuan, and the intelligent investment is 384.1 billion yuan, accounting for the total investment of the power grid. of 11.13% .

Many people who don't know much about the smart grid may not understand why the country is investing so much energy to build a smart grid . What is the impact of the construction of smart grids on our lives ?

To figure out this problem, we must first understand, what is the smart grid ?

The concept of smart grid has been put forward very early, not to mention Europe and the United States. Even domestically, smart grid has been set as the goal and direction of grid development. 2009 Nian 5 months, the State Grid Corporation of the " 2009 special international conference on high-voltage transmission technology" on the proposed called "strong smart grid " development planning.

So what is a smart grid ?

State Grid China Electric Power Research Institute Definition: Based on the physical grid ( China's smart grid is based on the UHV grid as the backbone grid, the strong grid of coordinated development of voltage grade grids ) , the modern advanced sensor measurement technology A new type of power grid formed by the high integration of communication technology, information technology, computer technology and control technology with the physical grid. It achieves reliable and economical users for the purpose of fully satisfying users' demand for electricity and optimizing resource allocation, ensuring the safety, reliability and economy of power supply, meeting environmental constraints, ensuring power quality, and adapting to the development of power market. Clean, interactive power supply and value-added services.

Ok, Xiao Bian admits that this concept is a bit confusing, and it will not be easy to understand for a while. But it doesn't matter, let's first take a look at what is the "smart" of the smart grid ? After the research, you may have a better understanding of the smart grid concept.

1

User interaction, grid transparency

How to understand this user interaction? In the past, users had almost one option for the grid: paying for electricity on time. Oh, maybe there is one, that is, the user can take a look at the meter and walk a few degrees this month. (The reason why "go" is used is because most old-fashioned electric meters are mechanical aluminum-plated meters.) Other than that, there seems to be really nothing to do.

Old-fashioned electricity meter and smart meter comparison

So what about smart grids ?

Users will be an integral part of the power system. Encouraging and facilitating users to participate in their own operations and management is an important feature of the smart grid .

For the power supply company, mastering the needs of users can better measure the relationship between supply and demand. By counting the user's electricity consumption information, the power supply company can understand the electricity usage rules in an area from the data analysis, such as when to use more electricity, and when to use less electricity, and accordingly develop energy-saving power generation in each region. Transmission and distribution solutions.

In addition, by adjusting the user's power consumption time, the power efficiency of the power grid terminal can be effectively improved, the peak filling valley is cut, the grid load curve is smoothed, and the grid load pressure is reduced. The benefit of this is that reducing or diverting electricity demand during peak hours allows utilities to minimize capital expenditures and operating expenses. The cost of electricity is reduced, and the price of electricity will naturally be lowered. (Well, I can only say that this is the case in theory.)

For the user, the power consumption is the same as the mobile phone bill, and can be selectively consumed. Users can choose different options to purchase power and choose electricity. For example, the user can check at any time, the electricity price is high during peak hours, then I will use it as little as possible; the electricity price in the low valley period is cheap, and it is matched with the intelligent electric appliance timing operation or remote control, and chooses to use electricity during the low electricity price period.

At the same time, users can also develop appropriate power usage plans based on this information. In some developed countries in Europe and America, this kind of electricity-using scheme has gradually matured, and it is possible to choose electricity by choosing the electricity-using scheme that suits oneself.

2

Smart devices to realize the Internet of Things

As mentioned above, smart terminals such as smart home appliances and intelligent control devices will occupy a very important position in the smart grid . By using the power APP installed on the mobile phone , it is possible to remotely control the electric water heater, air conditioner, refrigerator, electric kettle and other electrical appliances, and it is easy to use electricity when the electricity price is cheap.

Smart energy management system

I might as well imagine it. When going out in the winter, go to the air conditioner in advance through the APP before going home , and you can touch the warmth when you open the door. I want to burn a pot of water at half price before 6 o'clock in the morning , but I don't want to get up so early. I can use the APP timing function to ensure that the water is automatically completed before 6 o'clock... Through the APP , it can be like checking the flow of the mobile phone at any time. Understand the power consumption of an electrical device for a certain period of time, so that users can see at a glance their own electricity accounts.

The Smart Grid in Electric Energy collection box, not only just past the one-way function of the collection, they will bring their own WIFI and network functions, the user of the power of information, after collecting data, sent through the network to the power company Data terminal. The power supply company will classify and compare these data, and then tailor the power plan according to the actual situation of the user's electricity consumption, and send the notification through SMS and other forms.

For example, users can know which appliances in their homes are wasted by querying the real-time usage data of high-energy appliances. For example, if the TV is on but not seen, no one in the house can turn on the air conditioner and so on.
