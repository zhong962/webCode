Internet of Vehicles: Opportunities and Challenges Coexist

        Published: 2019-09-05         Big mean small

The Internet of Vehicles is a comprehensive electronic information technology. Each vehicle is used as an information source and connected to the network through wireless communication means, thus achieving unified management of vehicles nationwide. As the core technology of the Internet of Vehicles, the vehicle information system is widely used by OEMs and service providers in the functions of satellite navigation, car collision avoidance, road rescue, real-time communication, audio-visual entertainment and so on.

Four aspects reflect the value of the Internet of Vehicles

In the first place, the market space is large. Both domestic car ownership and sales volume are already in the forefront of the world, and some luxury luxury brands have moved their so-called base headquarters to China. The vehicle-based vehicle networking market capacity is huge, but the current domestic car network has just started, especially in the front-loading market. The vehicle companies have just built the service content related to the Internet of Vehicles, and there are very few domestic car network users. However, with the promotion of the market and the improvement of user awareness, the car network will explode in three years. The scale will not be less than 10 million, and the number of users will continue to surge.

The second aspect is closer to the user. Who is closest to users, manufacturers, 4S stores, all people who are car-serviced and rely on cars to make money are closest to users. The automobile industry chain is relatively solid, and the user's behavior, experience, car condition and other information is very valuable to the manufacturers, and other value-added services will be derived. The car network will gradually become a bridge between the car manufacturers and users.

The third aspect is to win the right to speak for the manufacturer. At present, the homogenization of automobile brand products is very serious, such as a four-wheel drive SUV, and each brand is similar. A compact economy car is also similar, even including the exterior. Vendors need to think about how to increase the value of their products through differentiation to attract consumers. Other aspects can be played very little. The automobile manufacturing industry is a very mature industry, but the Internet of Vehicles is a huge space that can be exerted.

In the fourth aspect, the cloud-plus model saves costs for vendors and vendors in the value chain. Car manufacturers are making cars. Whether they are understanding the mobile Internet or developing application software, they are inexperienced. Just the cloud plus this way provides them with a good solution, and the use of cloud technology can also reduce costs.

Four major problems need to be solved

The first is the payment issue. Mobile payments are becoming more mainstream, and people like to shop at online stores. China Mobile and UnionPay cooperate to promote NFC's low-end mobile phones. This year, 50 million units may be pre-installed in China, and short-term contacts will become mainstream in the future.

Followed by the network environment. Although Beijing's mobile network coverage is already good, the signal is still not satisfactory. For the Internet of Vehicles, the Internet is indispensable. The current network environment is still not fully satisfied for the Internet of Vehicles.

Again is the security and privacy of positioning. Most of the car networking services are location-related and are LBS services. Vehicle positioning is the foundation, but this location and mobile phone number, like the communication products such as mail and WeChat, are also one of the privacy, and it requires national legislation to become smooth.

Finally, there are technical issues. According to the technical problems of the Internet of Vehicles, there is still a certain gap between domestic and foreign countries. There are not many technical companies and developers involved in this aspect, and they are not mature enough. With the growing market size of the Internet of Vehicles and technological breakthroughs, it is not impossible for China's car networking technology to lead the world in the future.

In the information age, consumers are no longer satisfied with the car as a “transfer tool”, and the car will become the terminal for information reception. Therefore, the function of the in-vehicle information system is limited to “basic service”, which is not enough to extend to the field of life and entertainment, or even to meet Office needs. Through "cloud computing", you can enjoy Internet real-time search, maps, mail, audio-visual entertainment and other services on the car screen, perhaps not far away.
