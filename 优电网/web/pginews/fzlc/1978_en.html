Development status

The investment in power grid continues to grow steadily. The investment scale in 2017 is nearly 600 billion.

        Published: 2019-08-23         Big mean small

At the end of 1978, China's power generation installed capacity was 57.12 million kilowatts, and the power generation was 256.55 billion kilowatt hours, which is only equivalent to the current level of a province. Per capita installed capacity and per capita power generation are less than 0.06 kW and 270 kWh. Power generation capacity and power generation are only ranked eighth and seventh in the world respectively. The scale of power development at the beginning of reform and opening up is not only far below the world average, but also because the severe shortage has become a bottleneck restricting the development of the national economy.

At the beginning of reform and opening up, China's power technology level is relatively backward. China has only a small number of 200,000 kilowatts of thermal power units, and 300,000 kilowatts of thermal power units still need to be imported. Nuclear power plants were not built until the 1980s with the help of foreign countries. At the beginning of reform and opening up, the highest voltage level of China's power grid was 330 kV. In 1981, the first 500 kV ultra-high voltage transmission line—Henan Pingdingshan to Hubei Wuchang power transmission and transformation project was completed.

Reform and opening up has opened up the development of electric power construction. After nine years, by 1987, China’s power generation capacity reached the first 100 million kilowatts.

The first 10 years of reform and opening up mainly addressed the serious shortage of power supply. Carry out “financing and running electricity” to solve the problem of insufficient funds for power construction. The Ministry of Power proposed to use the department to cooperate with local and departments and departments to run electricity, raise funds and manage electricity, use foreign capital to run electricity, etc. to solve the problem of insufficient funds for power construction, and to verify the electricity price based on the principle of debt service. The level breaks the single electricity price model and fosters a mechanism for pricing according to market rules.
