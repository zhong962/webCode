Development status

China Electricity Council released "China Power Industry Annual Development Report 2019"

         Published: 2019-06-14        Bigmidsmall

　　In order to comprehensively and objectively reflect the development and reform of the power industry in 2018 and serve the scientific development of power industry enterprises, on June 14, the China Electricity Council (hereinafter referred to as “CEC”) held a press conference in Beijing to release “China”. Power Industry Annual Development Report 2019 (referred to as "Industry Development Report 2019").

　　The "China Power Industry Annual Development Report" was first launched in 2006. The CEC fully utilizes the power industry's statistical and investigative functions and supplements it with information provided by enterprises and related institutions. It is organized annually and is the first year of the power industry. The development report is also the first official publication that comprehensively reflects the overall development of the industry and the overall situation of the reform. It has been published for 14 years and has formed its own unique style and distinctive features. It is widely used by governments at all levels, power industry enterprises and all walks of life. Concerns, with greater influence and high reference value, have become an important carrier for CLP service members and all sectors of society, and have become important reference materials for electric power practitioners and readers who care about electric power industry to understand the development of China's electric power.

　　"Industry Development Report 2019" consists of 12 chapters. Through in-depth analysis and graphic presentation, it fully reflects the development of power industry, reform and market construction, investment and construction, production and supply, consumption and supply and demand, safety and reliability in 2018. Sex, green development, technology and informatization, power standardization, enterprise development and management, development trends and characteristics in the field of international exchanges and cooperation, highlighting the development achievements of the industry, proposing the main problems in the development of the industry, and making a moderate outlook. The appendix of “Industry Development Report 2019” lists relevant data such as the major events of the power industry in 2018, electricity prices, power production and construction, and development and operation of large-scale power companies.

　　"Industry Development Report 2019" pointed out that in 2018, under the strong leadership of the Party Central Committee with Comrade Xi Jinping as the core, the power industry was guided by Xi Jinping's new era of socialism with Chinese characteristics, and resolutely implemented the decision-making and deployment of the Party Central Committee and the State Council. The revolution and a new strategy for energy security, in accordance with the fundamental requirements of high-quality development, increase the power structure adjustment, continue to accelerate the development of clean energy, promote cross-regional cross-province power transmission, promote the comprehensive optimization of clean energy, and promote terminal energy. Electrification utilization level; deepen power system reform, vigorously promote power market construction, promote power science and technology innovation; further strengthen green development, actively respond to climate change; continuously expand "One Belt, One Road" power international cooperation, and continue to build a clean, low-carbon, safe and efficient modern Energy system. In 2018, the development of the power industry presents nine characteristics.

　　First, the power supply capacity continued to increase and the structure was further optimized.

　　Power generation capacity continues to increase. In 2018, the country's new power generation capacity (formally put into production) was 127.85 million kilowatts, which was 2.34 million kilowatts less than the previous year. Among them, hydropower is 8.59 million kilowatts (including 1.3 million kilowatts of pumped storage), which is the least new year for hydropower production since 2003; thermal power is 43.8 million kilowatts (including coal and electricity of 30.56 million kilowatts and gas power of 8.84 million kilowatts) , a decrease of 730,000 kilowatts compared with the previous year (of which coal-fired power decreased by 4.48 million kilowatts compared with the previous year, gas and electricity increased by 3.13 million kilowatts compared with the previous year), which has been reduced for four consecutive years; nuclear power is 8.84 million kilowatts, and nuclear power is put into a new high every year; Net wind power and solar power generation reached 22.27 million kilowatts and 45.25 million kilowatts, respectively, which invested 4.07 million kilowatts and operated less than 8.15 million kilowatts, respectively, which accounted for 52.0% of the newly added installed capacity in the country. By the end of 2018, the national installed capacity of full-caliber power generation was 19,012,000 kilowatts, an increase of 6.5% over the previous year, and the growth rate dropped by 1.2 percentage points from the previous year. Among them, hydropower 352.59 million kilowatts (including pumped storage power generation of 29.99 million kilowatts, an increase of 4.5% over the previous year), an increase of 2.6% over the previous year; thermal power of 114.408 million kilowatts (including coal and electricity 100.835 million kilowatts, an increase of 2.3% over the previous year) , an increase of 3.1% over the previous year; nuclear power of 44.66 million kilowatts, an increase of 24.7% over the previous year; grid-connected wind power of 184.27 million kilowatts, an increase of 12.4% over the previous year; grid-connected solar power generation of 174.33 million kilowatts, an increase of 33.7% over the previous year. The per capita installed capacity of the country is 1.36 kW/person, an increase of 0.08 kW/person over the previous year, ranking the world average for many years. The power generation and installation structure was further optimized. The installed capacity of non-fossil energy power generation in the country was 775.51 million kilowatts, accounting for 40.8% of the total installed capacity of the country's power generation, an increase of 2.1 percentage points over the previous year; the total installed capacity of new energy power generation was 38.06 million kilowatts, accounting for 18.9%. The number of single-unit 1 million kilowatt-class thermal power units reached 113, and the capacity of thermal power units of 600,000 kilowatts and above reached 44.7%.

　　The size of the grid has increased. In 2018, the new transmission line length of 110 kV and above and the capacity of substation equipment were 56,973 km and 31.24 million kVA, which were 1.9% and 4.8% lower than the previous year respectively; due to the national air pollution prevention action plan The transmission channels have been put into production one after another. The newly added direct current transmission lines and commutation capacity are 3,325 kilometers and 32 million kilowatts, respectively, down 61.3% and 59.5% from the previous year. By the end of 2018, the length of the transmission line of the national grid of 35 kV and above was 1.89 million kilometers, an increase of 3.7% over the previous year. Among them, the transmission line length of 220 kV and above is 730,000 km, an increase of 7.0% over the previous year. The national grid power grid of 35 kV and above has a capacity of 7 billion kVA, an increase of 5.4% over the previous year. Among them, 220 kV and above substation equipment capacity of 4.3 billion kVA, an increase of 6.0% over the previous year. In 2018, two DC-cross-region UHV lines were put into operation nationwide, with an additional trans-regional transmission capacity of 15 million kilowatts. By the end of 2018, the national trans-regional transmission capacity reached 136.15 million kilowatts, of which the transmission capacity of the AC-DC network was 12.281 million kilowatts; the transmission capacity of the inter-regional point-to-network was 13.34 million kilowatts.

　　Power investment fell. 2018, the nation's major power generation companies 1 power engineering construction investment 278.7 billion yuan, down 3.9% over the previous year, a decrease year on year last year narrowed. Among them, hydropower reached 70 billion yuan, an increase of 12.7% over the previous year (of which, pumped storage capacity was 16.2 billion yuan, up 14.0% over the previous year, which was the largest investment over the years); nuclear power was 44.7 billion yuan, down 1.6% from the previous year; Wind power was 64.6 billion yuan, down 5.2% from the previous year and has fallen for three consecutive years; solar power generation was 20.7 billion yuan, down 27.4% from the previous year. Thermal power investment was 78.6 billion yuan, down 8.3% from the previous year. The investment amount was the smallest in 2004. Among them, coal-fired power generation was 64.4 billion yuan, down 8.8% from the previous year, accounting for 23.1% of power investment, basically the same as last year. The investment in power generation was 14.2 billion yuan, which was basically the same as the previous year. The national power grid project completed an investment of 534 billion yuan, which was basically the same as last year. Among them, the DC project was 52 billion yuan, down 39.4% from the previous year, accounting for 9.7% of the total investment in power grid projects; the exchange project was 460 billion yuan, an increase of 7.6% over the previous year. The investment in distribution network increased rapidly, and the investment in distribution network was 302.3 billion yuan, an increase of 6.4% over the previous year.

　　Second, power production is growing rapidly, and production operations are safe and reliable.

　　Non-fossil energy power generation has accelerated. The national full-caliber power generation capacity was 699.47 billion kWh, an increase of 8.4% over the previous year, and the growth rate was 1.9 percentage points higher than the previous year. Among them, the hydropower generation capacity was 12,321 billion kWh, an increase of 3.1% over the previous year; the thermal power generation capacity was 494.29 billion kWh, an increase of 7.3% over the previous year. Among them, the coal-fired power generation capacity was 44,829 billion kWh, an increase of 7.3% over the previous year; The nuclear power generation capacity was 295 billion kWh, an increase of 18.9% over the previous year; the grid-connected wind power generation capacity was 365.8 billion kWh, an increase of 20.1% over the previous year; the solar power generation capacity was 176.9 billion kWh, an increase of 50.2% over the previous year. In 2018, the national non-fossil energy power generation amounted to 2,163.4 billion kWh, an increase of 11.1% over the previous year, accounting for 30.9% of the total-caliber power generation, an increase of 0.8 percentage points over the previous year, and a contribution rate of 40.0 to the national power generation growth. %; new energy power generation increased by 28.5%, contributing to the national power generation growth rate of 22.2%.

　　The annual coal price for power generation fluctuated at a high level. In 2018, the supply of coal was tight and slow, and the overall balance was achieved. The price of coal for power generation in the whole year was stable and stable. According to CECI's purchase price index CECI, the spot price fluctuation of 5500 kcal in 2018 is 560-752 yuan/ton, and the comprehensive price fluctuation range is 571-635 yuan/ton. The average comprehensive price exceeds the national average. The upper limit of the green range is stipulated, and the cost of purchasing coal for domestic coal-fired power companies remains high.

　　The problem of abandoning wind and falling light continues to improve. In 2018, with the concerted efforts of governments at all levels and the whole industry, the new energy power generation has further improved. In the whole year, the national abandoned wind power was 27.7 billion kWh, and the average abandoned wind rate was 7%, down 5 percentage points from the previous year. The national abandoned photoelectric output was 5.49 billion kWh, and the average light rejection rate was 3%, down 2.8 percentage points from the previous year.

　　The use of grid-connected wind power equipment hit the highest in 2013. Due to the higher growth rate of power consumption and less water in some river basins, the utilization hours of thermal power, nuclear power and new energy power generation increased more than the same period of last year. Among them, thermal power 4378 hours, an increase of 159 hours over the previous year (fire-burning power generation 4495 hours, an increase of 183 hours over the previous year; gas power generation 2767 hours, an increase of 6 hours over the previous year); nuclear power 7543 hours, an increase of 454 hours over the previous year; On-grid wind power was 2,103 hours, an increase of 155 hours over the previous year, the highest since 2013; solar power generation was 1,230 hours, an increase of 25 hours over the previous year; hydropower was 3,607 hours, an increase of 10 hours over the previous year.

　　Power production operation is safe and reliable. In 2018, there were no major electric accidents and deaths in the country, and there were no safety incidents of power system hydropower dams, dams, dams and major impacts on society. The main indicators of power reliability have remained at a high level. Among them, the equivalent availability coefficients of hydropower units of 40,000 kilowatts and above and coal-fired power units, gas turbines and nuclear power units of 100,000 kilowatts and above are 92.30%, 92.26%, 92.47% and 91.84% respectively. Except for the equivalent available coefficient of nuclear power plants increased by 0.74 percentage points, the others decreased by 0.25, 0.5 and 0.13 percentage points respectively compared with the previous year. The available coefficients of the three main types of power transmission and transformation facilities, such as overhead lines, transformers and circuit breakers, were 99.328%, 99.741%, and 99.908%, respectively, both lower than the previous year. The total energy availability and energy utilization rate of HVDC transmission systems were 92.15% and 44.11%, respectively, which were 3.20 and 10.31 percentage points lower than the previous year; the total forced outage was 35 times, an increase of 2 times over the previous year. The average power supply reliability rate of users in 10 (6, 20) kV power supply systems nationwide was 99.820%, an increase of 0.006 percentage points over the previous year; the average power outage time of users was 15.75 hours/household, a decrease of 0.52 hours/household from the previous year; the average power outage frequency of users 3.28 times / household, unchanged from the previous year.

　　Third, the whole society has experienced rapid growth in electricity consumption and overall balance of power supply and demand.

　　The whole society uses electricity to grow faster. Under the comprehensive influence of the overall stability of the macroeconomic operation, the rapid development of the service industry and high-tech and equipment manufacturing industry, the winter cold wave and the summer high temperature, the rapid replacement of electric energy, and the transformation of the urban rural power network, the whole society uses electricity. Faster growth. In 2018, the national total electricity consumption was 6.9002 billion kWh, an increase of 8.4% over the previous year. It was the highest growth rate since 2012, and the growth rate was 1.8 percentage points higher than the previous year. Among them, the electricity consumption of the first, second and third industries and urban and rural residents increased by 9.0%, 7.1%, 12.9% and 10.3% respectively over the previous year. The electricity consumption of the secondary industry is still the main force driving the growth of electricity consumption in the whole society, driving 5.0 percentage points, up 1.1 percentage points over the previous year; the tertiary industry and urban and rural residents continue to maintain rapid growth in electricity consumption for the whole society. The driving force for power growth increased by 0.5 and 0.4 percentage points respectively over the previous year.

　　The rapid replacement of electric energy. In 2018, the cumulative amount of replacement electricity was 155.76 billion kWh, an increase of 21.1% over the previous year. Among them, the national (agricultural) industry manufacturing field completed the replacement of electricity of 96.80 billion kWh, accounting for 62.2% of the total replacement power; residential heating, transportation, energy production supply and consumption, etc. are also rapidly promoted, alternative power Increase year by year.

　　New formats for power terminals are being cultivated. New formats for power terminals such as integrated energy, power system energy storage and electric vehicle charging have developed rapidly in terms of technology and commercial applications. As of the end of 2018, the first batch of 55 "Internet +" smart energy (energy Internet) demonstration projects, 23 multi-energy complementary integrated optimization demonstration projects and 28 new energy micro-grid demonstration projects announced by the National Energy Administration are being piloted. .

　　Power demand side management is further deepened. In 2018, government departments at all levels, industry associations, and power companies deepened their power demand side management. Through the active exploration and implementation of the demand side response, the localities have reduced the peak power by 12.45 million kilowatts. Power grid enterprises implemented measures to save power and reduce power consumption in various sectors of the society, and saved electricity by 16.3 billion kWh and saved 4.31 million kilowatts of electricity.

　　The overall situation of power supply and demand is balanced. In 2018, the national electricity supply and demand situation changed from the overall easing in the previous two years to the overall balance. Among them, the power supply capacity in the northeast and northwest regions is surplus; the overall supply and demand of electricity in North China, East China, Central China and South China is balanced; some areas are affected by factors such as large-scale rain and snow weather at the beginning of the year, continuous high temperature in summer, and tight fuel supply in some periods. The balance of power supply and demand during the period is tight.

　　Fourth, the level of green power development has been continuously improved, and new achievements have been made in energy conservation and emission reduction.

　　The level of resource conservation continues to increase. In 2018, the average coal consumption of coal-fired power plants of 6000 kW and above was 307.6 g/kWh, down 1.8 g/kWh from the previous year. The coal consumption of coal-fired power plants continued to be advanced in the world; the power consumption rate of the plant was 4.70%, compared with the previous year. The decline was 0.10 percentage points (of which hydropower was 0.26%, down 0.01 percentage points from the previous year; thermal power was 5.81%, down 0.23 percentage points from the previous year). The national line loss rate was 6.21%, down 0.27 percentage points from the previous year. The power consumption per unit of power generation in thermal power plants nationwide was 1.23 kg/kWh, down 0.02 kg/kWh from the previous year; the comprehensive utilization rates of fly ash and desulfurization gypsum were 71% and 74%, respectively, both down 1 percentage point from the previous year. The comprehensive utilization has continued to increase.

　　Pollutant emissions are further reduced. In 2018, the national emissions of electricity, dust, sulfur dioxide and nitrogen oxides were about 210,000 tons, 990,000 tons and 960,000 tons, respectively, which were about 19.2%, 17.5%, and 15.8% lower than the previous year. The amount of soot, sulfur dioxide and nitrogen oxides was about 0.04g, 0.20g and 0.19g, which were 0.02g, 0.06g and 0.06g respectively compared with the previous year. The thermal power unit discharge capacity of wastewater is 0.06 kg / kWh, which is the same as the previous year. By the end of 2018, the coal-fired generating units that reached the ultra-low emission limit were about 810 million kilowatts, accounting for 80% of the total installed capacity of coal power in the country. The eastern and central regions basically achieved ultra-low emission transformation, and Henan, Anhui, Gansu and other provinces advanced. Complete the goal of ultra-low emission transformation.

　　Carbon intensity continues to decrease. In 2018, the national unit thermal power generation carbon dioxide emissions were about 841 g / kWh, down 19.4% from 2005; the unit power generation carbon dioxide emissions were about 592 g / kWh, down 30.1% from 2005. Taking 2005 as the base year, in 2006-2018, through the development of non-fossil energy, reducing coal consumption and line loss rate, the power industry has reduced carbon dioxide emissions by about 13.68 billion tons, effectively slowing the growth of total carbon dioxide emissions. Among them, the reduction of coal consumption by power supply contributed 44% to the carbon dioxide emission reduction of the power industry, and the contribution rate of non-fossil energy development was 54%.

　　Fifth, new progress has been made in technological innovation, and the level of power construction and operation technology has continued to improve.

　　The technological innovation of the power industry in 2018 has effectively promoted the scientific and technological progress of the industry, and implemented the national green development strategy, showing the international competitiveness of core technologies with independent intellectual property rights. In the field of hydropower, it solved the technical problems of safety risk prevention and control of the high arch dam project, created the Chinese brand of hydraulic levee technology for high dam navigation, and mastered the world's leading manufacturing technology of the giant mega-hydrogen generating unit of one million kilowatts. In the field of thermal power, the key technologies for the near-zero loss heating of the cold end of steam turbines for thermal electrolysis are completed, and the natural gas distributed energy system and the pre-combustion CO 2 capture technology of IGCC have achieved core technologies with independent intellectual property rights. In the field of nuclear power, we have mastered the fourth-generation nuclear power high-temperature gas-cooled reactor steam generator manufacturing technology; in the field of new energy power generation, relying on the Shanghai Donghai Bridge offshore wind power demonstration project to build China's first large-scale offshore wind farm, in solar thermal power generation technology, new high-efficiency solar energy Significant progress has been made in battery technology research. In the field of power grid, it has overcome the worldwide problem of automatic voltage control of complex power grids, invented the key technologies of power grid control for large-scale mountain fire disasters in the power grid, innovated the key technologies of controllable ice melting in power grids, and comprehensively overcome key cores such as ±1000 kV UHV DC transmission. Technology, successfully developed the world's first UHV GIL equipment and achieved mass production.

　　Sixth, the standardization work was solidly promoted, and new progress was made in the internationalization of standards.

　　The pace of standard revision has been further accelerated. Upon approval, a total of 492 power-related standards were issued throughout the year. Among them, there are 49 national standards, 323 industry standards, 46 industry standards in English, and 74 CEC standards.

　　The international influence of electric vehicle charging facilities standards has been further enhanced. China Light and Power and Japan's Electric Vehicle Fast Charger Association signed a memorandum of understanding on technology and standards for electric vehicle charging facilities, and launched cooperation between China and Japan in the field of electric vehicle charging facilities. The electric vehicle charging roaming information led by China Telecom. The first part of the International Series of Interactions: General Requirements (IEC 63119-1) was first raised to international standards.

　　Seventh, power reform continued to advance and the power market accelerated construction.

　　Complete the transmission and distribution price verification for the first cycle. In 2018, on the basis of establishing a framework for the regulation of transmission and distribution prices covering provincial power grids, regional power grids, inter-provincial trans-regional transmission projects, local power grids, and incremental distribution networks, North China, Northeast China, East China, Central China, The two-part transmission and distribution price of the first cycle of the five major regional power grids in the northwest and the transmission prices of 24 inter-provincial and inter-regional special projects have been verified. The cumulative reduction of the permitted revenue of the grid enterprises is about 60 billion yuan, which promotes cross-provincial cross-regional power transactions. After the first period of 4 years, the transmission and distribution pricing cost supervision and auditing was completed.

　　Exceeded the task of reducing the general industrial and commercial electricity price by 10%. The parties actively implemented the spirit of the central government's reform on the supply side and specifically implemented the goal of reducing the general industrial and commercial electricity price by 10% throughout the year. Through the central government to reduce the national major water conservancy project construction fund collection standards, release tax reduction dividends, grid enterprises to promote regional power grid and cross-regional cross-provincial special transmission price reform, flexible implementation of two-part electricity prices, local government multi-channel self-raising and other 10 price cuts The cleaning measures reduced the cost of electricity for users by 125.791 billion yuan in four batches, with an average decrease of 0.0789 yuan/kWh. The national average fell by 10.11%.

　　Continuously improve the various policies for the pilot reform of the incremental power distribution business. Through organizing supervision and investigation, and sorting out the problems of the first three batches of incremental power distribution pilot projects, the National Development and Reform Commission and the National Energy Administration successively issued policy documents on power business licensing and distribution area division, which accelerated the incremental allocation. The electric pilot has an orderly landing process, and at the same time, the fourth batch of pilot project declaration work has been deployed.

　　The proportion of market trading power has increased significantly. In 2018, the number of market participants participating in the market in the country has further expanded. The power consumption plans for coal, steel, nonferrous metals and building materials have been released, and the scale of electricity consumption in the national electricity market has reached a new level. Total annual electricity trading 2 2.0654 trillion kwh, up 26.5 percent over the previous year. The market transaction power accounted for 30.2% and 37.1% of the total electricity consumption of the whole society and the sales volume of power grid enterprises, respectively, an increase of 4.3 percentage points and 5 percentage points respectively over the previous year.

　　The pilot construction of the power spot market was steadily advanced. In accordance with the requirements of the National Development and Reform Commission and the National Energy Administration to accelerate the pilot work of power spot, in August 2018, the southern spot (starting from Guangdong) started the pilot operation of the power spot market and became the first power spot market in China to be put into trial operation. In order to promote the construction of the power spot market as soon as possible to achieve substantive breakthroughs, the National Development and Reform Commission and the Energy Bureau established a contact coordination mechanism for eight pilots. The Gansu and Shanxi power spot markets started trial operation in December.

　　Eighth, the growth rate of total assets of power companies fell, and the debt ratio decreased year-on-year.

　　The growth rate of total assets decreased. According to the statistics of the National Bureau of Statistics, as of the end of 2018, the total assets of power enterprises above designated size were 1,400.8 billion yuan, an increase of 2.7% over the previous year, and the growth rate decreased by 1.8 percentage points over the previous year. Among them, the total assets of power supply enterprises increased by 4.2% over the previous year; the total assets of power generation enterprises increased by 1.5% over the previous year (of which, the total assets of thermal power enterprises decreased by 1.8% compared with the previous year).

　　Corporate debt ratios have fallen. The asset-liability ratio of power enterprises above designated size was 60.2%, down 1.1 percentage points from the previous year. Among them, the asset-liability ratio of power supply enterprises decreased by 1.6 percentage points compared with the previous year, and the asset-liability ratio of power generation enterprises decreased by 0.6 percentage points from the previous year. The decrease in the asset-liability ratio of power generation enterprises was mainly due to the decrease in the asset-liability ratio of solar power generation, wind power, hydropower and nuclear power enterprises by 1.5, 1.4, 1.3 and 0.9 percentage points respectively. The asset-liability ratio of thermal power enterprises was the same as that of the previous year.

　　The profit of power supply companies has declined. The total profit of power enterprises above designated size was 323.1 billion yuan, an increase of 3.1% over the previous year. Among them, the power supply enterprises implemented the 2018 government work report proposed in the general industrial and commercial electricity price reduction of 10% on average, after the profit to the electricity companies, profits fell 24.3% over the previous year. Driven by the low base in the previous year and the rapid growth of power generation, power generation enterprises realized a total profit of 221 billion yuan, an increase of 23.8% over the previous year. Among them, thermal power generation enterprises realized a profit of 32.3 billion yuan, but the loss is still high (43.8%); wind power, solar power and nuclear power profit growth rate exceeded 20.0%, but wind power, solar power generation due to subsidies are not in place, corporate books It is difficult to convert profits into actual capital flows in the short term, which makes the capital turnover difficult.

　　Nine is the acceleration of the global energy Internet, and the “One Belt, One Road” power cooperation has presented new highlights.

　　Actively carry out "One Belt, One Road" cooperation. In 2018, China's major power companies participated in the “One Belt, One Road” international cooperation and actually completed an investment of about 2.8 billion US dollars, involving 8 countries along Asia and Europe, directly creating 6,700 local jobs. There are a total of 128 newly signed engineering contracting projects involving 30 countries and regions along the line with a contractual value of US$25.55 billion. In order to ensure the smooth implementation of the “Belt and Road” construction, all power companies have continuously innovated the “One Belt, One Road” construction cooperation mechanism and established a common investment risk prevention system in consultation with relevant countries or regions.

　　The global energy Internet is accelerating. In 2018, the number of members and coverage countries of the Global Energy Internet Cooperation Organization increased significantly. 242 new members were added throughout the year, 40 new countries were added, and the total number of members reached 602, covering 85 countries (regions); Internet University Alliance and Think Tank Alliance, and established a new platform for cooperation and development; jointly initiated with the Guinean government to establish the African Energy Internet Sustainable Development Alliance; with the United Nations Climate Change Convention Secretariat, the Economic Commission for Latin America and the Caribbean, the Economic Commission for Africa , Ethiopia, Congo (Kinshasa), Guinea and other governments, as well as related organizations, institutions, enterprises, universities and new 15 cooperation agreements; vigorously promote China's interconnection with neighboring countries and the "One Belt and One Road" national grid, China-Myanmar Meng networking project A joint intergovernmental working group was established, the China-South Korea networking project completed the pre-feasibility study, and the Gulf countries and the East Africa networking project signed a tripartite cooperation agreement.

　　"Industry Development Report 2019" also pointed out that in 2018, while China's power industry has achieved outstanding results, some deep-seated problems accumulated over the years still exist. In terms of development, problems such as uncoordinated development of power supply and power grid, AC and DC, and transmission and distribution are prominent; clean energy development has long faced challenges such as abandonment of water, abandonment of wind, and abandonment of light, which seriously restricts the safe development, clean development and high power of the power industry. Quality development; the task of carbon emission reduction and pollution prevention facing coal power development is arduous. In terms of reform and innovation, the reform of state-owned enterprises' "distribution management" lags behind the process of market-oriented construction, which is not conducive to the innovative development of new production and operation of energy production and consumption. In terms of operations, affected by macroeconomics, electricity price policies, environmental governance, etc., the operating situation of power companies is still grim.

　　"Industry Development Report 2019" suggests that the power industry should adhere to the guidance of Xi Jinping's new era of socialism with Chinese characteristics, continue to accelerate the development of energy, green and low-carbon transformation, promote the implementation of clean alternatives and electric energy substitution, significantly increase the level of electrification, and create green and low carbon. , interconnected, and jointly build a shared energy community. Accelerate the large-scale development and efficient use of clean energy. Effectively solve the problems of development and consumption, accelerate the development of large-scale clean energy bases, adhere to the centralized and distributed development of clean energy, and rely on the large power grid to achieve efficient use of distributed power. Actively promote the transformation and development of coal power. Strictly control the scale of new coal power, optimize the layout of coal power, and improve the capacity of coal power regulation. Continue to promote the transformation and development and innovation of power companies. Focusing on energy transformation, we will identify the direction of enterprise development, realize the transformation of the main business into a clean and low-carbon field as soon as possible, realize the transformation from extensive to lean, continue to improve the level of lean management, increase efforts to expand the international market, and actively promote technical equipment and business. Model innovation promotes traditional power companies to expand their integrated energy services business. Focus on solving the dilemma of power generation enterprises to prevent market risks. We will do a good job in the adjustment of the benchmark price of electricity and coal, the supply of fuel, the price of stabilized coal, and the supervision of the implementation of the coal-to-coal coalition, to alleviate the operational difficulties of power generation enterprises.
